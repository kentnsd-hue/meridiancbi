Visa-Free Travel Compared: Which CBI Passport Actually Gets You Where in 2026?

July 05, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

By Meridian Advisory | Updated June 2026

Not all second passports are created equal.

When high-net-worth individuals explore Citizenship by Investment, the conversation almost always starts with the same question: "Where can I actually travel with this passport?"

It's the right question. Visa-free access is one of the most tangible, immediate benefits of a second citizenship — and the differences between programs can be dramatic. A passport that opens 145 doors is a fundamentally different asset than one that opens 120.

Let's break it down.

The 2026 Visa-Free Travel Scoreboard

Here's how the most popular CBI passports stack up for visa-free and visa-on-arrival access as of mid-2026, based on the latest Henley Passport Index and verified travel advisories:

| CBI Program | Visa-Free / VOA Destinations | Schengen Access | UK Access | China Access | Key Regions of Strength |

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Malta | ~170+ | ✅ (EU member) | ✅ | Visa required | Europe, Americas, Asia-Pacific |

| Portugal Golden Visa | ~190+ | ✅ (EU member) | ✅ | ✅ | Near-universal access |

| St. Kitts & Nevis | ~157+ | ✅ (90-day) | ✅ (180-day eVisa/ETA) | Visa required | Schengen, UK, Americas, parts of Asia |

| Grenada | ~148+ | ✅ (90-day) | ✅ | ✅ (30-day) | Schengen, UK, China, Americas |

| Dominica | ~145+ | ✅ (90-day) | ✅ | Visa required | Schengen, UK, Americas, CARICOM |

| Antigua & Barbuda | ~150+ | ✅ (90-day) | ✅ | Visa required | Schengen, UK, Americas |

| Vanuatu | ~112+ | ✅ (90-day) | ✅ | Visa required | Schengen, UK, limited Asia |

| Türkiye | ~115+ | ❌ | ✅ | Visa required | Central Asia, Middle East, parts of Americas |

| Jordan | ~55+ | ❌ | Visa required | Visa required | Middle East, select Africa |

Note: Numbers are approximate and shift regularly as bilateral agreements are updated. Portugal provides a pathway to citizenship (not immediate citizenship), while Malta's program leads to naturalization after a residency period.

What the Numbers Don't Tell You

Raw destination counts are useful — but sophisticated travelers and business owners need to think beyond the headline number. Here's what actually matters:

1. Schengen Zone Access Is the Great Equalizer

Almost every serious CBI passport — from St. Kitts to Malta — grants visa-free entry to the Schengen Zone, covering 27 European countries. This is often the single most valuable corridor for entrepreneurs and investors.

The difference? EU passports (Malta, Portugal) let you live and work there indefinitely. Caribbean passports give you 90 days out of every 180.

If you need occasional European travel for meetings, conferences, and leisure, a Caribbean passport handles that beautifully. If you want to relocate to Europe, you need an EU pathway.

2. China Access: The Grenada Advantage

Here's where Grenada punches far above its weight. It's one of the only Caribbean CBI passports that provides visa-free access to China (30 days).

For investors and entrepreneurs with business ties to mainland China, this is not a minor detail — it's a strategic differentiator worth potentially hundreds of hours in avoided visa applications over a lifetime.

3. The US E-2 Treaty: Grenada's Other Hidden Edge

Grenada holds a unique position among Caribbean CBI programs: it maintains an E-2 Investor Visa treaty with the United States. This means Grenada citizens can apply for a renewable US investor visa that allows them to live, work, and manage a business in the US.

No other Caribbean CBI program offers this pathway. For anyone with US business ambitions who doesn't qualify for (or want to wait for) a traditional US immigration route, Grenada is the strategic play.

4. UK Access Is Shifting — Stay Current

Post-Brexit, UK travel rules for Caribbean passport holders have evolved. Most Caribbean CBI passports still grant visa-free access or streamlined electronic travel authorization to the UK, but the terms vary and are subject to change. Always verify the latest bilateral arrangements before making a decision based on UK access alone.

5. Vanuatu: Fast but Limited

Vanuatu remains the fastest CBI program in the world — citizenship can be granted in as little as 30–60 days. But its passport trails behind in raw travel power, particularly in Asia and the Americas.

If speed is your priority and your travel needs are primarily Europe-focused, Vanuatu makes sense. If you need broad global access, it's a compromise.

The Real Framework: Matching Passport to Lifestyle

At Meridian Advisory, we advise clients to stop chasing the highest number and start asking better questions:

→ "Where do I actually need to go — this year and in the next decade?"

→ "Am I optimizing for travel, for tax residency, or for a Plan B?"

→ "Do I need to live somewhere, or just visit freely?"

The answers reshape the conversation entirely.

Here's a simplified decision framework:

🌍 You Want Maximum Global Access, Including EU Residency Rights

→ Malta or Portugal

These are premium programs with higher investment thresholds and longer timelines, but they unlock EU residency and work rights — a categorically different asset than visa-free tourist access. Portugal's Golden Visa pathway, even after its 2023 restructuring, remains one of the most respected routes to EU citizenship for investors.

Investment range: €300K–€690K+ depending on the route

Timeline to citizenship: 5+ years (Portugal), 12–36 months (Malta)

🇺🇸 You Want a Bridge to the United States

→ Grenada

The E-2 treaty access is unmatched in the CBI world. Combine that with China access, Schengen access, and UK access, and Grenada is arguably the most strategically versatile Caribbean passport available in 2026.

Investment: From $235,000 (donation) or $270,000+ (real estate)

Timeline: 4–6 months

⚡ You Want Speed and Schengen Access at the Best Value

→ St. Kitts & Nevis or Dominica

The two oldest and most established Caribbean programs. St. Kitts carries slightly more prestige and a few more visa-free destinations. Dominica typically comes in at a lower price point. Both deliver strong Schengen and UK access with processing times of 3–6 months.

Investment: From $100,000 (Dominica donation) to $250,000+ (St. Kitts real estate)

Timeline: 3–6 months

🏎️ You Need a Passport Yesterday

→ Vanuatu

Fastest in the world. Limited in scope compared to Caribbean options, but if you need a travel document with Schengen access in 30–60 days, there's no alternative.

Investment: From $130,000 (donation)

Timeline: 30–60 days

A Word on Due Diligence and Program Integrity

Travel access is only as durable as the program's reputation. Programs that cut corners on due diligence risk losing visa-free agreements — we've seen this play out repeatedly over the past decade.

This is why we always advise clients to weigh program integrity and longevity alongside raw passport power. A passport from a program with rigorous vetting and strong international standing is a safer long-term asset than one from a program optimizing purely for speed and volume.

St. Kitts, Grenada, and Malta consistently rank among the most respected programs globally for exactly this reason.

The Bottom Line

Your second passport is a decades-long asset. The right choice depends not just on where you can travel today, but on where the world is heading — and where your life, family, and business interests are taking you.

The wrong approach? Picking a passport based on a single number on an index.

The right approach? A strategic conversation about your actual goals, travel patterns, family situation, and long-term plans.
