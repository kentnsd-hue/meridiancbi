Why Grenada's CBI Program Is the Ultimate Gateway to the US Market for Entrepreneurs

June 17, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

The Hidden Path to America That Most Entrepreneurs Don't Know About

Every year, thousands of entrepreneurs around the world dream of building a business in the United States — the world's largest consumer market, its deepest talent pool, and its most robust startup ecosystem. But the reality of US immigration is brutal. H-1B visa lotteries, EB-5 backlogs stretching a decade or more, and ever-shifting policy winds make a direct path to the US feel nearly impossible for many founders.

There is, however, a remarkably elegant workaround that savvy global entrepreneurs are increasingly leveraging in 2026. It starts not in New York or Silicon Valley, but on a 133-square-mile island in the Caribbean.

It starts with Grenada.

The E-2 Treaty Visa: America's Best-Kept Immigration Secret

The E-2 Treaty Investor Visa is a non-immigrant US visa that allows citizens of certain treaty nations to live and work in the United States by investing in and directing a US-based business. Unlike the EB-5 investor green card — which currently requires a minimum investment of $800,000 (or $1,050,000 outside Targeted Employment Areas) and faces years-long processing queues — the E-2 visa offers a faster, more flexible, and significantly more affordable route.

Here's what makes the E-2 so compelling:

| Feature | E-2 Treaty Visa | EB-5 Investor Green Card |

|---|---|---|

| Minimum Investment | No statutory minimum (typically $100K–$200K+) | $800,000 – $1,050,000 |

| Processing Time | 2–6 months (often weeks at consulates) | 2–10+ years |

| Renewability | Indefinitely renewable in 2- or 5-year increments | Permanent (once approved) |

| Family Coverage | Spouse + children under 21 (spouse can work) | Spouse + children under 21 |

| Job Creation Requirement | No strict statutory requirement (must show business is "real and operating") | Must create 10 full-time US jobs |

| Path to Green Card | Not direct, but strategies exist | Direct path to permanent residency |

The catch? Your country of citizenship must have an E-2 treaty with the United States. And this is precisely where Grenada enters the picture.

Why Grenada — and Only Grenada — Bridges CBI and E-2 Access

There are dozens of Citizenship by Investment programs available worldwide. Several Caribbean nations offer them — St. Kitts & Nevis, Dominica, Antigua & Barbuda, and St. Lucia among them. But there is one critical distinction that separates Grenada from every other Caribbean CBI program:

Grenada is the only Caribbean CBI nation that maintains an E-2 Treaty with the United States.

This single fact transforms Grenada's citizenship from a "nice-to-have" travel document into a strategic business asset — a key that unlocks legal residency and business operations in the world's largest economy.

For entrepreneurs from countries that don't have an E-2 treaty with the US — including China, India, Brazil, South Africa, the UAE, Russia, and dozens of others — Grenada's CBI program represents perhaps the most efficient lawful pathway to establishing a physical presence in the American market.

How It Works: The Grenada-to-US Pipeline, Step by Step

The process is more straightforward than most people expect:

Step 1: Obtain Grenadian Citizenship Through Investment

Grenada offers two primary investment routes:

  • National Transformation Fund (NTF) Donation: A non-refundable contribution starting at $235,000 for a single applicant (as of 2026, subject to program updates). Family applications are available at scaled pricing.
  • Approved Real Estate Investment: A minimum investment of $270,000 in a government-approved real estate project, held for a minimum of 3–5 years depending on the project.

Processing time: Approximately 4–6 months from application to passport in hand.

Step 2: Apply for the US E-2 Visa as a Grenadian Citizen

Once you hold your Grenadian passport, you are eligible to apply for an E-2 Treaty Investor Visa at a US consulate. You will need to demonstrate:

  • A substantial investment in a bona fide US enterprise (no statutory minimum, but most immigration attorneys recommend $100,000+ to build a strong case)
  • That the business is real, active, and not marginal — meaning it has the capacity to generate income beyond just supporting you and your family
  • That you will direct and develop the enterprise

Step 3: Live, Work, and Build in the United States

Upon E-2 approval, you and your family can relocate to the US. Your spouse receives work authorization. Your children can attend US schools. You run your business. The visa is renewable indefinitely as long as the business continues operating.

Total realistic timeline from Day 1 to US residency: 6–12 months.

Compare that to the EB-5's multi-year wait and million-dollar price tag, and the strategic advantage becomes clear.

The Numbers That Are Driving This Trend in 2026

Grenada's CBI program has seen significant interest growth, and the E-2 treaty connection is the primary driver. Consider:

  • E-2 visa approvals have historically numbered in the tens of thousands annually, with the visa category maintaining one of the highest approval rates among US non-immigrant visa classes.
  • Countries without E-2 treaties represent some of the world's largest entrepreneurial populations. China (1.4 billion people, no E-2 treaty), India (1.4 billion, no E-2 treaty), Brazil (215 million, limited treaty access) — the demand pool is enormous.
  • The cost calculus is favorable. A Grenada NTF donation of ~$235,000 plus an E-2 business investment of ~$150,000 totals roughly $385,000 — less than half the minimum EB-5 threshold, with a fraction of the processing time.
  • Grenada's passport itself offers visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 140+ countries, including the UK, EU Schengen Zone, China, and Singapore — making it a powerful mobility tool independent of the E-2 pathway.

Beyond the E-2: The Full Strategic Value of Grenadian Citizenship

While the E-2 treaty is the headline attraction, Grenadian citizenship delivers value across multiple dimensions:

🌍 Global Mobility

Access to 140+ destinations without a visa or with visa-on-arrival. For entrepreneurs from countries with weak passport rankings, this alone can save hundreds of hours annually in visa applications and travel friction.

🛡️ Plan B & Political Hedge

In an increasingly unpredictable geopolitical landscape, a second citizenship provides optionality. It's insurance — not against a specific scenario, but against uncertainty itself.

💼 Tax Planning Opportunities

Grenada has no personal income tax on worldwide income, no capital gains tax, no wealth tax, and no inheritance tax. While tax obligations depend heavily on where you reside (and US tax obligations apply while living in the US on an E-2), the structure offers planning flexibility for globally mobile entrepreneurs.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Inclusion

Grenada's CBI application can include a spouse, children, parents, grandparents, and siblings under certain conditions — making it one of the most family-inclusive programs available.

🏫 Education Access

Grenadian citizens benefit from access to certain scholarship and tuition programs, including at St. George's University, a well-regarded institution with a strong medical school that feeds graduates into US residency programs.

  • E-commerce and Amazon/Shopify entrepreneurs looking to legitimize and scale their US operations with physical presence
  • Crypto and Web3 founders seeking a compliant, structured way to operate in the US market

Common Questions We Hear

"Can I eventually get a US green card through the E-2?"

The E-2 visa itself does not directly lead to a green card. However, there are well-established strategies — including employer sponsorship (EB-1/EB-2/EB-3), the EB-5 program (pursued concurrently while on E-2 status), or marriage to a US citizen — that can bridge the gap. Many clients use the E-2 as a "bridge" while pursuing permanent residency through a parallel track.

"Is this legal and above-board?"

Absolutely. The E-2 Treaty between the United States and Grenada is a formal bilateral agreement. US consulates routinely process E-2 applications from naturalized Grenadian citizens who obtained citizenship through the CBI program. There is no prohibition on this pathway.

"Will the treaty or the CBI program go away?"

No one can guarantee the permanence of any government policy. However, the US-Grenada E-2 treaty has been in place since 1989, and Grenada's CBI program has operated since 2013 with increasing institutional maturity. The strongest argument for acting sooner rather than later is precisely this uncertainty — the window is open now.

"What if my E-2 application is denied?"

Denial rates for well-prepared E-2 applications are low, but preparation is critical. The business plan, investment evidence, and application documentation must meet a high standard. This is why working with experienced CBI and US immigration counsel — in coordination — is essential.

Why Timing Matters in 2026

Several converging trends make 2026 a particularly strategic moment to pursue this pathway:

1. US immigration policy remains restrictive for most employment-based categories, with no major reform on the horizon. The E-2 remains one of the few relatively accessible pathways.

2. CBI program pricing tends to increase over time. Grenada has adjusted its contribution thresholds upward in recent years, and further increases are possible as demand grows.

3. Global instability continues to drive demand for second citizenship. From geopolitical tensions to currency volatility to pandemic-era travel restrictions that remain fresh in memory, the case for optionality has never been stronger.

4. Increased regulatory scrutiny across all CBI programs means that due diligence standards are rising. Applicants with clean profiles should act while processing remains relatively straightforward.
