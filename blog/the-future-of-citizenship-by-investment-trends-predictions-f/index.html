The Future of Citizenship by Investment: Trends & Predictions for 2027

April 28, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | 2026

The citizenship by investment (CBI) landscape has never been more dynamic. As we move through 2026, the forces reshaping global mobility — geopolitical instability, digital wealth creation, evolving tax frameworks, and a post-pandemic rethinking of what "home" means — are accelerating at a pace few predicted even two years ago.

At Meridian Advisory, we work with high-net-worth individuals every day who are navigating these shifts in real time. Here's what we see coming in 2027 — and what it means for anyone considering a second citizenship.

1. Due Diligence Standards Will Become the New Differentiator

The days of "fast and loose" CBI processing are over. Throughout 2025 and 2026, we've watched Caribbean programs — particularly St. Kitts & Nevis — invest heavily in enhanced due diligence frameworks, biometric screening, and inter-governmental intelligence sharing.

Our prediction for 2027: Programs with the strongest vetting processes will command the most respect on the global stage — and their passports will carry greater visa-free access as a direct result. We expect at least one Caribbean nation to announce a formal due diligence partnership with an EU or Five Eyes intelligence body.

What this means for you: Choosing a program is no longer just about cost or speed. The credibility of the program you select will directly impact the long-term value of your passport. Cut-rate programs with weak oversight are a liability, not a bargain.

2. Minimum Investment Thresholds Will Continue Rising

This trend has been building for years, and it isn't slowing down. St. Kitts raised its minimum contribution in recent years. Grenada has tightened its pricing. Portugal's Golden Visa has shifted away from real estate in Lisbon and Porto. Malta's program already sits at the premium end of the market.

Our prediction for 2027: Expect at least two major programs to announce threshold increases in the first half of the year. The era of sub-$150,000 citizenship options from reputable jurisdictions is drawing to a close.

What this means for you: If you've been on the fence, the math is straightforward. Every year you wait is likely to cost you — not just in higher investment minimums, but in stricter qualification criteria and longer processing timelines. The window of opportunity isn't closing, but it is narrowing.

3. The "Crypto Citizen" Will Become a Formal Category

Digital asset holders have been among our fastest-growing client segments throughout 2026. Founders who held through the last cycle, early DeFi participants, NFT creators, and DAO contributors are seeking jurisdictions that offer regulatory clarity, favorable tax treatment, and genuine acceptance of crypto-sourced wealth.

Our prediction for 2027: At least one CBI program will formally announce a dedicated pathway for digital asset holders — with tailored source-of-funds documentation requirements, partnerships with blockchain analytics firms like Chainalysis, and potentially even acceptance of crypto for investment contributions.

What this means for you: If your wealth is primarily on-chain, the compliance environment is actually moving in your favor — but only if you get ahead of it. Working with an advisory team that understands both CBI and crypto compliance is critical.

4. The EU Will Tighten — But Not Eliminate — Its Golden Visa Options

The European Commission has been vocal about its skepticism toward investment migration. But here's what the headlines miss: member states with golden visa programs generate significant foreign direct investment, and the political will to shut them down entirely simply isn't there.

Our prediction for 2027: Portugal will further refine its Golden Visa to focus on fund-based investments and contributions to underserved regions. Malta will maintain its premium-tier Individual Investor Programme with modest adjustments. Greece's program, after a series of changes, will stabilize at a higher threshold. And we may see a newer EU member state quietly introduce a residency-by-investment track.

What this means for you: European residency and citizenship pathways remain viable, but they increasingly reward sophisticated investors who can navigate complexity. Cookie-cutter applications will face higher rejection rates. Tailored strategy matters more than ever.

5. "Plan B" Will Become "Plan A" for a Growing Segment

Perhaps the most profound shift we're witnessing isn't regulatory — it's psychological. In 2026, we're having conversations with clients who no longer view second citizenship as an insurance policy. They view it as a primary strategic asset: a tool for structuring international businesses, accessing new markets, optimizing tax exposure, and giving their families genuine optionality.

Our prediction for 2027: The stigma around investment migration will continue to erode. Second citizenship will be discussed openly in the same circles where estate planning, asset protection, and international tax strategy are standard conversation. We expect mainstream financial advisory firms to begin offering CBI referrals as a formal part of their wealth management services.

What this means for you: You're not early anymore — but you're not late, either. The individuals who move now will lock in programs at current thresholds, benefit from faster processing, and have their global infrastructure in place before the next wave of demand drives timelines longer.

6. Family-Centric Applications Will Dominate

Throughout 2026, we've seen a decisive shift: the majority of our applications now include spouses, children, and increasingly, aging parents and adult siblings where programs allow it.

Our prediction for 2027: Programs will expand dependent eligibility to capture this demand. Expect more flexible definitions of "dependent" and the introduction of multi-generational packages. St. Kitts and Grenada are most likely to lead here.

What this means for you: If you're thinking about citizenship for yourself alone, think bigger. The per-person marginal cost of adding family members is often remarkably low compared to the standalone cost — and the protective value of ensuring your entire family has global mobility is incalculable.

7. Processing Times Will Diverge Sharply Between Programs

As application volumes grow, programs will split into two camps: those that invest in operational capacity and technology to maintain fast processing, and those that buckle under demand.

Our prediction for 2027: Top-tier programs like St. Kitts will push toward 45–60 day standard processing. Others may stretch to 9–12 months. The gap between "well-run" and "overwhelmed" will be wider than ever.

What this means for you: Speed has real value — especially if your motivation for second citizenship involves time-sensitive business expansion, geopolitical risk, or an upcoming liquidity event. Program selection should factor in operational track record, not just headline promises.
