The Future of Citizenship by Investment: Trends & Predictions for 2027

July 07, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

The citizenship by investment (CBI) landscape has never been more dynamic — or more consequential. As we move through 2026, the forces reshaping global mobility are accelerating: geopolitical fragmentation, evolving tax frameworks, digital-first economies, and a generation of entrepreneurs who think in passports, not borders.

At Meridian Advisory, we work at the intersection of these trends every day. Here's our data-driven outlook on where CBI is heading in 2027 — and what it means for high-net-worth individuals considering a second citizenship.

1. Due Diligence Will Become the New Differentiator

The era of "fast-track, no-questions-asked" citizenship is definitively over.

Throughout 2025 and into 2026, we've watched international bodies — the OECD, the EU, and the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) — tighten scrutiny on CBI programs. Countries that fail to demonstrate rigorous vetting processes face real consequences: correspondent banking losses, visa-free travel downgrades, and reputational damage.

Prediction for 2027: Programs that invest heavily in multi-layered due diligence — biometric screening, source-of-funds verification, and cross-jurisdictional background checks — will emerge as the "gold standard." Expect St. Kitts & Nevis and Grenada to continue leading here, having already implemented some of the industry's most robust compliance frameworks.

What this means for you: The application process may take longer, but the value of the passport you receive will be stronger. A citizenship obtained through a rigorous program carries more weight with banks, immigration authorities, and business partners worldwide.

2. The Rise of "Strategic Citizenship" Over "Backup Citizenship"

For years, the primary motivation for CBI applicants was a Plan B — a safety net against political or economic instability. That narrative is shifting.

In 2026, we're seeing a marked increase in applicants who aren't fleeing anything. They're building something. Entrepreneurs structuring multi-jurisdictional businesses. Crypto investors seeking regulatory clarity. Remote-first founders optimizing for tax efficiency, travel access, and quality of life simultaneously.

Prediction for 2027: "Strategic citizenship" will become the dominant framework. Advisors and applicants alike will evaluate programs not just on passport strength, but on:

  • Tax treaty networks — Does this citizenship unlock favorable double taxation agreements?
  • Crypto and digital asset regulation — Is the jurisdiction forward-thinking on blockchain?
  • Business incorporation ease — Can you efficiently set up and operate a company?
  • Banking access — How readily can you open personal and corporate accounts?

What this means for you: Choosing the right program isn't just about the number of visa-free countries anymore. It's about aligning your citizenship portfolio with your business model, investment strategy, and lifestyle goals.

3. Portugal's Golden Visa Will Continue to Evolve — and Remain in Demand

Portugal's Golden Visa program has been one of the most talked-about residency-by-investment pathways in recent years. After the 2023 reforms eliminated real estate as a qualifying investment route, many predicted the program's decline. They were wrong.

The pivot toward fund-based investments, scientific research contributions, and cultural heritage projects has actually attracted a more sophisticated applicant pool. In 2026, Portugal remains one of the most compelling pathways to EU residency — and eventually citizenship — for investors willing to play the long game.

Prediction for 2027: Expect Portugal to introduce further refinements that balance economic development goals with investor appeal. We anticipate expanded qualifying fund categories and potentially streamlined processing times for applicants who meet enhanced due diligence thresholds. The pathway from Golden Visa to Portuguese citizenship (typically five years) will remain one of the most cost-effective routes into the EU.

What this means for you: If EU access is part of your strategy, Portugal's program deserves serious consideration now — before potential cap restrictions or minimum investment increases take effect.

4. Caribbean Programs Will Consolidate and Professionalize

The Caribbean CBI market is maturing. The five traditional programs — St. Kitts & Nevis, Dominica, Grenada, Antigua & Barbuda, and St. Lucia — have spent the last several years navigating price wars, regulatory pressure, and the constant balancing act between volume and integrity.

In 2026, we're seeing a clear divergence: programs that prioritize governance and transparency are pulling ahead, while those perceived as less rigorous face increasing headwinds.

Prediction for 2027: We expect to see:

  • Minimum investment thresholds stabilize or increase across all Caribbean programs, reducing the race-to-the-bottom dynamic.
  • Regional coordination on due diligence standards, possibly through a unified Caribbean CBI regulatory body.
  • Grenada's unique value proposition — its E-2 treaty with the United States — will continue to drive outsized demand from investors seeking a stepping stone to the U.S. market.
  • St. Kitts & Nevis, as the oldest and most established program, will leverage its brand equity and pioneer status to attract applicants who prioritize prestige and reliability.

What this means for you: Not all Caribbean citizenships are created equal. The program you choose in 2027 will signal something about your standards and intentions to banks, business partners, and immigration authorities for decades to come.

5. The Middle East and Central Asia Will Enter the Arena

While Caribbean and European programs dominate today's CBI conversation, new entrants are emerging. Several Middle Eastern and Central Asian nations are studying — and in some cases quietly developing — investment migration frameworks.

Countries seeking to diversify away from resource-dependent economies see CBI and residency-by-investment (RBI) programs as powerful tools for attracting foreign capital, entrepreneurial talent, and global connectivity.

Prediction for 2027: At least one or two new programs from the Middle East or Central Asian region will formally launch or enter advanced pilot stages. These programs will likely emphasize:

  • Business establishment and job creation over passive investment
  • Technology sector development
  • Regional travel mobility within emerging economic blocs

What this means for you: New programs present both opportunities and risks. Early movers can secure favorable terms, but unproven programs carry uncertainty around long-term passport strength and international recognition. Working with experienced advisors becomes even more critical.

6. Digital Nomad Visas and CBI Will Converge

The digital nomad visa movement — which exploded from 2020 to 2024 — is evolving into something more permanent. Many professionals who started with temporary remote work visas are now seeking long-term residency or citizenship in their adopted countries.

Prediction for 2027: We'll see hybrid programs that offer a clear escalation path: digital nomad visa → permanent residency → citizenship, with investment thresholds at each stage. Countries that already have both DNV and CBI frameworks (like Portugal, Grenada, and Antigua) are best positioned to capture this demand.

What this means for you: If you're currently on a digital nomad visa or considering one, think two steps ahead. The right initial move can set you on a pathway to permanent citizenship — with the right guidance.

7. Family-Centric Applications Will Dominate

In 2026, we're already seeing a significant trend: CBI applicants are increasingly motivated by their families, not just themselves. Parents seeking better educational access for their children. Multi-generational families looking to secure mobility options for aging parents. Families hedging against regional instability by diversifying their "citizenship portfolio."

Prediction for 2027: Programs that offer the most inclusive dependent policies — covering spouses, children, parents, grandparents, and siblings — will capture disproportionate market share. Expect processing frameworks to become more family-friendly, with bundled application fees and streamlined documentation for multi-generational submissions.

What this means for you: When evaluating programs, don't just think about your own passport. Consider the full picture: Who in your family benefits? What are the age limits for dependents? Can you add family members after the initial application?

What Does This All Mean for You?

The CBI industry in 2027 will be more sophisticated, more regulated, and more strategically valuable than ever before. The days of treating a second passport as a luxury novelty are gone. For globally minded entrepreneurs and investors, citizenship planning is becoming as fundamental as tax planning or estate planning.

But more options and more complexity also mean more room for costly mistakes — choosing the wrong program, missing regulatory changes, or failing to align your citizenship strategy with your broader financial picture.

That's exactly where expert guidance makes the difference.
