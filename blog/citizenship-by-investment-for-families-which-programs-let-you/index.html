Citizenship by Investment for Families: Which Programs Let You Include Parents, Siblings, and Extended Relatives?

July 10, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

When high-net-worth individuals explore second citizenship, the question is rarely just about themselves. It's about their families — their spouse, their children, their aging parents, and in many cases, their siblings who share the same desire for global mobility and security.

The good news? Several Citizenship by Investment (CBI) programs are designed with families in mind. The challenging part? Each program defines "family" differently, and the differences can mean tens of thousands of dollars in additional costs — or the exclusion of loved ones entirely.

In this guide, we break down which CBI programs offer the most generous family inclusion policies in 2026, so you can make an informed decision that protects your entire family unit.

Why Family Inclusion Matters in CBI Planning

A second citizenship isn't just a travel document. For families, it represents:

  • Collective security — a Plan B for every member, not just the primary applicant
  • Educational access — children and younger siblings gain access to schools and universities in new jurisdictions
  • Healthcare coverage — particularly relevant for aging parents
  • Estate and succession planning — simplified generational wealth transfer across borders
  • Unified travel freedom — the ability to move together without visa complications

Leaving a parent or sibling behind in a CBI application isn't just emotionally difficult — it can be strategically shortsighted.

Program-by-Program Breakdown: Who Can You Include?

🇰🇳 St. Kitts & Nevis

One of the most family-friendly CBI programs in the world.

St. Kitts & Nevis has long been considered the gold standard of Caribbean CBI, and its family inclusion policy is a major reason why.

Eligible dependents include:

  • Spouse
  • Children under 31 (if financially dependent on the main applicant)
  • Parents and grandparents aged 55+
  • Siblings of the main applicant — unmarried, childless, and under 30

St. Kitts is one of the few programs globally that explicitly allows siblings, which makes it exceptionally attractive for applicants from large families or those who want to secure a younger sibling's future.

Investment minimum: Starting at $250,000 (Sustainable Island State Contribution — SISC) for a family of up to four. Additional dependents incur supplementary fees.

Processing time: Typically 60–90 days via the Accelerated Application Process (AAP).

🇬🇩 Grenada

The best option for families who also want U.S. investor access.

Grenada's CBI program stands out for two reasons: its generous dependent policy and its E-2 Treaty with the United States, which allows Grenadian citizens to apply for a U.S. investor visa — a benefit that extends to the whole family.

Eligible dependents include:

  • Spouse
  • Children under 30 (if enrolled in higher education and financially dependent)
  • Parents and grandparents aged 55+
  • Siblings of the main applicant — unmarried, childless, and under 18

While Grenada does allow siblings, the age cutoff is notably lower than St. Kitts (under 18 vs. under 30). This is an important distinction for families with adult siblings.

Investment minimum: $235,000 (National Transformation Fund) for a family of four, or $270,000+ for the real estate option.

Processing time: Approximately 90–120 days.

Key advantage: The E-2 Treaty pathway to the U.S. is a game-changer for families with business interests in North America.

🇦🇬 Antigua & Barbuda

Strong family option with a unique five-day residency requirement.

Antigua offers a competitive family-inclusive CBI program with one distinctive requirement: at least one family member must spend a minimum of five days in Antigua within the first five years of citizenship.

Eligible dependents include:

  • Spouse
  • Children under 30 (if financially supported by the applicant)
  • Parents and grandparents aged 55+
  • Siblings of the main applicant — unmarried, childless, and under 30

Antigua's sibling policy mirrors St. Kitts in generosity, making it another excellent choice for applicants who want to include younger family members.

Investment minimum: Starting at $230,000 (National Development Fund) for a family of four.

Processing time: 90–120 days.

🇩🇲 Dominica

The most affordable option — but more limited on siblings.

Dominica consistently ranks as one of the most cost-effective CBI programs globally, but its family inclusion policy is slightly narrower than its Caribbean neighbors.

Eligible dependents include:

  • Spouse
  • Children under 30 (if financially dependent)
  • Parents and grandparents aged 65+ (note the higher age threshold)

Siblings: Dominica does not currently include siblings as eligible dependents in its CBI program.

Investment minimum: Starting at $200,000 (Economic Diversification Fund) for a family of four — the lowest entry point among major CBI programs.

Processing time: Approximately 90 days.

Best for: Smaller, nuclear family units where cost efficiency is a priority.

🇲🇹 Malta

Europe's premier CBI — premium pricing, premium benefits, stricter family definitions.

Malta's Citizenship by Naturalisation for Exceptional Services by Direct Investment (commonly known as the Malta CBI) is the most rigorous and prestigious program in Europe. It comes with full EU citizenship, which means freedom to live, work, and study across all 27 EU member states.

Eligible dependents include:

  • Spouse
  • Children under 29 (if unmarried and financially dependent)
  • Parents and grandparents who are genuinely dependent on the main applicant (no strict age minimum, but dependency must be proven)

Siblings: Malta does not include siblings.

Investment minimum: The total cost for a family typically ranges from €700,000 to over €1,000,000, including contributions to the national fund, property purchase or rental, and a philanthropic donation.

Processing time: 12–36 months, reflecting extensive due diligence.

Best for: Families seeking EU citizenship and who are willing to invest significantly for the highest-tier passport.

🇵🇹 Portugal Golden Visa

Residency-to-citizenship pathway — flexible for families, but requires patience.

Portugal's Golden Visa is technically a residency program that leads to citizenship after five years, but its family inclusion provisions are worth noting.

Eligible dependents include:

  • Spouse or partner
  • Children under 18 (or older if dependent and enrolled in education)
  • Parents of both the main applicant and the spouse, if aged 55+ and financially dependent

Siblings: Not included.

Investment minimum: Starting at €500,000 for qualifying investment funds (note: real estate options in Lisbon, Porto, and other high-density areas have been restricted since 2023).

Path to citizenship: 5 years of legal residency with minimal physical presence requirements (approximately 7 days per year).

Best for: Families taking a longer-term approach who want EU residency and eventual citizenship, particularly those looking to include parents from both sides of the family.

Strategic Considerations for Families

1. Don't Just Count Heads — Count Costs

Every additional dependent increases your total investment. Some programs charge a flat fee per dependent; others use a tiered structure. The "cheapest" program on paper may not be the most affordable once you add four or five family members.

2. Age Cutoffs Are Non-Negotiable — Timing Matters

If you have a child approaching 30 or a sibling approaching 18 (for Grenada), delaying your application by even a few months could disqualify them. Start the process early.

3. Consider Future Generations

Citizenship obtained through CBI is typically inheritable. Your children — and their children — can pass down the citizenship. This makes CBI one of the most enduring investments a family can make.

4. Pair Programs Strategically

Some families pursue two citizenships simultaneously. For example, Grenada for the U.S. E-2 Treaty access and Portugal for EU residency. When planned correctly, this gives a family coverage across North America, the Caribbean, and Europe.

5. Due Diligence Applies to Everyone

Every family member included in a CBI application undergoes background checks. A single red flag from any dependent can jeopardize the entire application. It's critical to work with an experienced advisory team to vet your application before submission.

The Bottom Line

If your priority is maximum family inclusion — especially siblings — St. Kitts & Nevis and Antigua & Barbuda lead the field with the most generous dependent policies in 2026.

If you want U.S. market access for your entire family, Grenada is the strategic choice.

If you're focused on European citizenship and long-term generational planning, Malta and Portugal remain the gold standard, despite their higher costs and longer timelines.

But every family is different. The right program depends on your family's size, ages, financial goals, travel needs, and long-term vision.
