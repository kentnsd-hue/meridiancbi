Antigua and Barbuda CBI: The Best Citizenship by Investment Program for Large Families in 2026

July 16, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Why savvy families are choosing this Caribbean twin-island nation — and saving tens of thousands in the process.

Introduction: When Family Size Becomes a Strategic Advantage

For high-net-worth individuals with large families, the math behind Citizenship by Investment (CBI) rarely works in their favor. Most programs charge steep per-dependent fees that can inflate total costs well past the $250,000+ mark for a family of five or more.

Antigua and Barbuda is the notable exception — and in 2026, it remains the single most cost-effective CBI program on the planet for families with four or more dependents.

If global mobility, generational wealth planning, and a Plan B passport are on your radar, here's why Antigua deserves to be at the top of your list.

The Numbers: Why Antigua Wins on Value for Families

Antigua and Barbuda's National Development Fund (NDF) donation route uses a pricing structure that's uniquely favorable to larger families:

| Family Size | NDF Donation (USD) | Per-Person Effective Cost |

|---|---|---|

| Single Applicant | $230,000 | $230,000 |

| Family of 4 | $240,000 | $60,000 |

| Family of 5 | $240,000 | $48,000 |

| Family of 6 | $240,000 | $40,000 |

| Family of 7+ | $240,000 + nominal fees | Even lower |

The key insight: A family of four pays only $10,000 more than a solo applicant. Adding a fifth, sixth, or even seventh family member to the application costs a fraction of what competing programs charge.

How This Compares to Other Programs

Let's put this in perspective for a family of six:

  • St. Kitts & Nevis: ~$250,000+ with dependent add-on fees
  • Grenada: ~$250,000+ (scaled dependent pricing)
  • Dominica: ~$250,000+ with incremental dependent fees
  • Malta: €750,000+ (an entirely different tier)
  • Antigua & Barbuda: ~$240,000 all-in via NDF

For large families, the savings can easily reach $30,000–$60,000 compared to otherwise comparable Caribbean programs. That's not a rounding error — it's a meaningful allocation that could go toward real estate, education, or further diversification.

Who Qualifies as a Dependent?

Antigua's dependent eligibility rules are among the most generous in the CBI space:

  • Spouse of the main applicant
  • Children under 30 (including those in university)
  • Parents and grandparents aged 55+ of either the main applicant or spouse
  • Siblings of the main applicant — unmarried, childless, and under 30

That last category is rare. Very few CBI programs allow siblings on an application, making Antigua a standout choice for applicants who want to extend mobility benefits across their broader family unit.

In practice, this means a three-generation application is entirely possible: grandparents, the main applicant and spouse, adult children, and even qualifying siblings — all on a single filing.

Beyond Cost: What Makes Antigua's Program Compelling in 2026

Price is the headline, but it's not the whole story. Here's what else makes Antigua a top-tier choice:

🌍 Visa-Free Travel to 150+ Destinations

Antigua and Barbuda passport holders enjoy visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to over 150 countries and territories, including:

  • United Kingdom
  • Schengen Zone (all 27 European countries)
  • Hong Kong & Singapore
  • Brazil, Colombia, and most of Latin America
  • Africa and the Middle East (select countries)

For families who travel frequently — whether for business, education, or lifestyle — this is a transformative upgrade from many passport limitations.

🏫 Educational Opportunities

Antigua is home to the University of the West Indies Five Islands Campus, and CBI citizens gain access to CARICOM educational agreements. For families with university-age children, this opens doors across the Caribbean and Commonwealth.

🏝️ Residency Flexibility

Antigua requires just five days of physical presence within the first five years of citizenship — the lowest residency requirement of any CBI program globally. For busy families who aren't ready to relocate, this is a critical advantage. You get the passport without uprooting your life.

💼 Tax Environment

Antigua and Barbuda levies no personal income tax, no capital gains tax, no wealth tax, and no inheritance tax. For families engaged in generational wealth transfer, this tax framework is a powerful planning tool — particularly when combined with proper structuring advice.

🏗️ Real Estate Option

Families who prefer a tangible asset over a donation can opt for the real estate route, investing a minimum of $300,000 in a government-approved development. This investment can be resold after five years, effectively recovering a significant portion of the outlay. Some families find this particularly appealing when they want a vacation property in the Caribbean that doubles as an investment.

Processing Timeline

In 2026, Antigua's Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU) is processing applications efficiently:

  • Standard processing: 3–4 months
  • Accelerated processing: Available for an additional fee, with approvals possible in as little as 45–60 days

For families facing time-sensitive needs — whether related to business travel, school enrollment deadlines, or geopolitical concerns — the accelerated option provides genuine peace of mind.

Due Diligence: What to Expect

Antigua maintains a rigorous due diligence process, which is actually a positive signal. Programs with strong vetting standards tend to maintain better international standing, which directly protects the long-term value of your passport.

Every applicant aged 16 and above undergoes background checks conducted through international databases and third-party verification firms. Clean criminal records, legitimate source of funds, and transparent financial histories are non-negotiable.

Our perspective at Meridian Advisory: We view strong due diligence as a feature, not a bug. The programs that cut corners on vetting are the ones that end up on EU watchlists and lose visa-free access. Antigua's thoroughness is an investment in the passport's future value.

Common Questions from Large Families

Q: Can I add family members after the initial approval?

Yes. Antigua allows you to add qualifying dependents to your citizenship after the initial grant — for example, a newborn child, a newly married spouse, or a parent who has since turned 55. Additional government fees apply, but the process is straightforward.

Q: Do all family members need to travel to Antigua?

No. Only the main applicant needs to fulfill the five-day presence requirement within the first five years. Dependents are not required to visit, though many families choose to combine the requirement with a vacation.

Q: Is dual citizenship permitted?

Absolutely. Antigua places no restrictions on holding multiple citizenships, and the process is entirely confidential. Your current country of citizenship will not be notified.

Q: What happens to my children's citizenship when they turn 30?

Once granted, citizenship is for life. The age limit applies only at the time of application. A child approved at age 28 remains a citizen indefinitely and can pass citizenship to their own future children.

Is Antigua the Right Fit for Your Family?

Antigua and Barbuda isn't the right program for everyone. If your primary goal is U.S. treaty investor (E-2) visa access, Grenada is the better choice. If European residency is the priority, Portugal or Malta should be on your shortlist.

But if you're a family of four or more looking for:

✅ The lowest total cost among reputable CBI programs

✅ Visa-free access to 150+ countries including the UK and Schengen

✅ Minimal physical presence requirements

✅ A tax-neutral jurisdiction for wealth planning

✅ The ability to include parents, grandparents, and even siblings

Then Antigua and Barbuda is, objectively, the most compelling option available in 2026.
