Global Mobility in Your Next Chapter: Why Retired Executives Are Investing in Second Citizenship

July 18, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

You spent decades building empires, navigating boardrooms, and making decisions that shaped industries. Now, you've stepped back from the C-suite. The corner office is someone else's. The calendar is finally yours.

But here's what many retired executives quickly discover: the world doesn't get smaller after retirement — it gets bigger.

And your single passport? It might be the one thing holding you back from fully living the next chapter you've earned.

The Post-Retirement Reality Check

Retirement for a former Fortune 500 CEO or serial entrepreneur doesn't look like a rocking chair on a porch. Today's retired executives are:

  • Sitting on international advisory boards
  • Managing diversified global investment portfolios
  • Splitting time between two or three countries
  • Exploring philanthropy across borders
  • Pursuing passion projects that span continents

Yet despite a lifetime of global influence, many retirees find themselves tethered by visa restrictions, tax inefficiencies, and limited healthcare access tied to a single citizenship.

A 2025 Henley & Partners report found that 73% of ultra-high-net-worth individuals either hold or are actively pursuing a second citizenship or residency. Among those over 55, the number has surged by 42% since 2022.

The message is clear: global mobility isn't just for jet-setting founders anymore. It's a strategic retirement asset.

Why Second Citizenship Makes More Sense Now Than Ever

1. Tax Optimization for Your Wealth Preservation Phase

During your earning years, aggressive tax planning was a competitive advantage. In retirement, it's a preservation strategy.

Many CBI-friendly jurisdictions offer:

  • Zero capital gains tax (St. Kitts & Nevis, Grenada)
  • No wealth or inheritance tax — critical for estate planning
  • Territorial tax systems — only income earned locally is taxed
  • No worldwide income reporting in certain jurisdictions

For a retired executive with a diversified portfolio — equities, real estate, private equity stakes, crypto holdings — the right second citizenship can mean the difference between preserving generational wealth and watching it erode through compounding tax obligations.

2. Global Healthcare Access

Let's be frank: healthcare becomes a priority, not an afterthought, in your 60s and beyond.

A second citizenship in the EU — through programs like Portugal's Golden Visa or Malta's Exceptional Investor Naturalization — unlocks access to world-class public and private healthcare systems consistently ranked among the best globally.

Portugal, for example, ranks 12th globally in the World Health Organization's healthcare system rankings, with exceptional specialist care at a fraction of US costs. Malta ranks 5th.

Compare that to navigating the complexities of Medicare while trying to spend six months a year in the Mediterranean. Citizenship solves what travel insurance never could.

3. Visa-Free Travel Without the Hassle

You've logged millions of miles. You know the frustration of visa applications, embassy appointments, and entry restrictions — even with a strong passport.

Second citizenship can dramatically expand your mobility:

| Passport | Visa-Free Destinations (2026) |

|---|---|

| St. Kitts & Nevis | 157 countries |

| Grenada | 148 countries |

| Malta | 190+ countries |

| Portugal | 191 countries |

A Grenada passport also uniquely provides access to the US E-2 Treaty Investor Visa — valuable if you want to maintain a footprint in the United States while holding Caribbean citizenship.

For the retired executive who wants to winter in Lisbon, spend spring in Singapore, and summer in the Caribbean — without paperwork headaches — a second passport isn't a luxury. It's logistics.

4. Estate Planning and Generational Wealth Transfer

Perhaps the most overlooked benefit: what you leave behind.

A second citizenship isn't just for you. It extends to your spouse, dependent children, and in many programs, even parents and grandchildren. You're not just buying a passport — you're gifting your family permanent optionality.

Consider:

  • St. Kitts & Nevis allows inclusion of dependents up to age 30 and parents over 55
  • Malta grants EU citizenship that passes to future generations
  • Grenada permits family inclusion with broad dependent definitions

In a world of rising geopolitical uncertainty, giving your children and grandchildren the ability to live, work, study, and invest across multiple jurisdictions is arguably the most valuable inheritance you can provide — more enduring than any trust fund.

The Most Popular Programs for Retired Executives in 2026

🇰🇳 St. Kitts & Nevis — The Gold Standard

  • Minimum investment: $250,000 (Sustainable Island State Contribution)
  • Timeline: 3–6 months
  • Why executives love it: Fastest processing, longest-running program (since 1984), zero income tax, and a strong reputation in global financial circles

🇬🇩 Grenada — The Strategic Choice

  • Minimum investment: $235,000 (National Transformation Fund)
  • Timeline: 4–6 months
  • Why executives love it: Only Caribbean CBI with US E-2 visa treaty access, making it ideal for those maintaining US business interests post-retirement

🇵🇹 Portugal Golden Visa — The European Gateway

  • Minimum investment: €500,000 (qualifying fund investments)
  • Timeline: Residency immediate; citizenship pathway in ~5 years
  • Why executives love it: EU citizenship, world-class lifestyle, path to full Portuguese (and therefore EU) citizenship for the entire family

🇲🇹 Malta — The Premium European Option

  • Minimum investment: €690,000+ (combined contribution, property, and donation)
  • Timeline: 12–36 months
  • Why executives love it: Direct EU citizenship, English-speaking, exceptional healthcare, and a robust financial services ecosystem for managing international investments
