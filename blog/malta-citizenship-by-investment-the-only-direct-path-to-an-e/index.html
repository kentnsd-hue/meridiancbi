Malta Citizenship by Investment: The Only Direct Path to an EU Passport in 2026

June 17, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Why Malta's Individual Investor Programme Remains the Gold Standard for Global Mobility

If you've been researching second citizenship with a focus on Europe, you've likely encountered a frustrating reality: most European residency-by-investment programs offer just that — residency. Not citizenship. Not a passport. A long road of renewals, physical presence requirements, and uncertain timelines before you can even apply for naturalization.

Malta is the exception.

The Malta Citizenship by Naturalisation for Exceptional Services by Direct Investment — commonly known as the Malta CBI program — is currently the only pathway in the European Union that leads directly to full citizenship and an EU passport through investment.

Let's break down exactly why that matters, how the program works, and who it's designed for.

Why an EU Passport Is the Ultimate Asset

Before diving into program specifics, it's worth understanding what an EU passport actually unlocks:

  • Freedom of movement across 27 EU member states — live, work, study, and retire anywhere in the European Union without restrictions
  • Visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 180+ countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Japan, Australia, and Singapore
  • Access to Europe's banking, business, and real estate markets on the same terms as any EU citizen
  • World-class healthcare and education systems across the continent
  • Generational wealth transfer — EU citizenship passes to your children and, in many cases, future descendants
  • Political and economic stability — a hedge against volatility in your home country

In 2026, with rising geopolitical uncertainty, currency instability in emerging markets, and increasing restrictions on travel for many passport holders, an EU passport isn't a luxury. It's a strategic asset.

How the Malta CBI Program Works

Malta's program is administered by Community Malta Agency under a rigorous legal framework that has been vetted and approved by the European Commission. It is capped at 1,500 successful applicants (excluding dependents), ensuring exclusivity and protecting the integrity of Maltese citizenship.

Investment Requirements

The program has three mandatory components:

#### 1. Contribution to the National Development and Social Fund (NDSF)

  • €750,000 if applying via the 12-month residency route
  • €600,000 if applying via the 36-month residency route

This is a non-refundable contribution to Malta's national development fund, which finances projects in healthcare, education, infrastructure, and social welfare.

#### 2. Real Estate Investment

Applicants must either:

  • Purchase residential property in Malta valued at a minimum of €700,000, OR
  • Lease residential property at a minimum annual rent of €16,000

The property must be held for a minimum of five years from the date of the citizenship certificate.

#### 3. Philanthropic Donation

A minimum €10,000 donation to a registered Maltese philanthropic, cultural, sport, scientific, animal welfare, or artistic non-governmental organization or society.

| Route | Contribution | Property (Purchase) | Property (Lease/yr) | Donation | Approx. Total |

|-------|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|----------|---------------|

Note: Additional government fees, due diligence fees, and legal/advisory costs apply.

The Application Process: What to Expect

Malta's CBI program is deliberately rigorous. This is a feature, not a bug — it's what gives the Maltese passport its credibility and what ensures the program's longevity within the EU framework.

Step 1: Eligibility Assessment & Engagement

Before any formal application, a thorough pre-screening is conducted to assess whether you meet the program's eligibility criteria. This includes a clean criminal record, a legitimate source of funds, good health, and no prior visa refusals to Malta or other EU states.

Step 2: Residency Permit

You must first obtain a Maltese residency permit. This is a genuine residency requirement — applicants must hold an e-residence card and establish a genuine link with Malta.

Step 3: Application Submission

A comprehensive application is filed with Community Malta Agency, including all supporting documentation, background information, and evidence of investment commitments.

Step 4: Four-Tier Due Diligence

This is where Malta separates itself from less credible programs. The due diligence process involves:

1. Community Malta Agency's own internal review

2. A third-party international due diligence firm (typically one of the world's leading risk consultancies)

3. Malta's Security Services

4. Interpol and international database checks

The rejection rate is meaningful. Malta has publicly denied applications that didn't meet its standards, which is precisely why the passport carries the weight it does.

Step 5: Residency Period

Depending on the route chosen, you must hold Maltese residency for either 12 months or 36 months before citizenship can be granted.

Step 6: Oath of Allegiance & Citizenship Certificate

Upon approval and completion of the residency period, you take an oath of allegiance to Malta and receive your citizenship certificate. You can then apply for your Maltese (EU) passport.

Malta vs. Other European Investment Migration Programs

Let's put Malta in context against the other major European programs available in 2026:

| Program | What You Get | Path to Citizenship | Timeline |

|---------|-------------|--------------------|---------|

| Malta CBI | Direct citizenship | Yes — by investment | 14–38 months |

| Portugal Golden Visa | Residency permit | Possible after ~5 years via naturalization | 6+ years |

| Greece Golden Visa | Residency permit | Possible after 7 years | 8+ years |

| Spain Golden Visa | Program closed (2025) | N/A | N/A |

| Italy Investor Visa | Residency permit | Possible after 10 years | 11+ years |

The difference is stark. Portugal's Golden Visa — an excellent program in its own right — requires years of residency, physical presence, Portuguese language proficiency, and a separate naturalization application with no guaranteed outcome. Malta offers a defined, contractual pathway to citizenship with clear requirements and a predictable timeline.

Common Questions We Hear

Does Malta allow dual citizenship?

Yes. Malta fully recognizes dual citizenship. You are not required to renounce your existing nationality.

Do I have to live in Malta?

You must hold genuine residency during the qualifying period (12 or 36 months) and establish a genuine link with the country. However, this does not mean you need to relocate your life there permanently. After citizenship is granted, there is no ongoing physical presence requirement to maintain your citizenship.

Is the program at risk of being shut down?

Malta's program operates under EU-approved guidelines and has withstood legal and political scrutiny at the European level. The capped nature of the program (1,500 applicants) and rigorous due diligence process have been key to its sustainability. That said, the landscape of investment migration is always evolving — which is precisely why acting sooner rather than later is advisable.

Can my family be included?

Yes. Your spouse, dependent children (including adult children up to a certain age), and dependent parents can be included in a single application.

What are Malta's tax implications?

Malta has an attractive tax regime, including a non-domicile tax status option for new residents, no inheritance tax, no wealth tax, and an extensive network of double taxation treaties. However, tax planning should be tailored to your individual circumstances — this is something we address in detail during consultations.
