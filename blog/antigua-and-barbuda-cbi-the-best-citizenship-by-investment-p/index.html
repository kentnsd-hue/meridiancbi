Antigua and Barbuda CBI: The Best Citizenship by Investment Program for Large Families in 2026

May 04, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Why this twin-island nation remains the top choice for families seeking second citizenship together — without breaking the bank.

Introduction: When Your Family Is Your Greatest Asset

For high-net-worth families exploring second citizenship, one question always surfaces early in the conversation: "Can we include the whole family?"

It's a fair question — and a critical one. Most Citizenship by Investment (CBI) programs allow a spouse and minor children. But what about adult children in university? Aging parents? Siblings you support financially?

This is exactly where Antigua and Barbuda separates itself from the pack.

While programs like St. Kitts and Malta have their own compelling advantages, Antigua and Barbuda has deliberately positioned its CBI program as the most family-inclusive option on the market. If you're a business owner, investor, or entrepreneur with a large or multi-generational household, this Caribbean nation deserves your full attention.

Let's break down why.

A Quick Overview of the Antigua and Barbuda CBI Program

Launched in 2013, Antigua and Barbuda's CBI program offers citizenship to qualified applicants who make a qualifying economic contribution to the country. In return, successful applicants and their eligible dependents receive:

  • Full citizenship and a passport from Antigua and Barbuda
  • Visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 150+ destinations, including the UK, EU Schengen Zone, Hong Kong, and Singapore
  • No residency requirements beyond a minimum of 5 days in the country within the first 5 years
  • No personal income tax, capital gains tax, or wealth tax
  • The right to live, work, and study in Antigua and Barbuda and across CARICOM member states

The program is regulated by the Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU) and has undergone significant due diligence enhancements in recent years, strengthening its international credibility.

Why Antigua and Barbuda Is the Gold Standard for Families

1. The Most Generous Dependent Policy in CBI

Here's where Antigua truly shines. Under the current 2026 program guidelines, a single application can include:

  • Spouse of the main applicant
  • Children under 18
  • Children aged 18–30 who are financially dependent on the main applicant (including those enrolled in higher education)
  • Parents and grandparents aged 55+ of the main applicant or spouse
  • Siblings of the main applicant who are unmarried, childless, and financially dependent

That last point is a game-changer. Very few CBI programs in the world allow siblings to be added as dependents. For families from cultures where extended family units are the norm — across the Middle East, South Asia, Africa, and East Asia — this is an enormous advantage.

In practical terms: A family of six, seven, or even eight members can often be covered under a single application, dramatically reducing the per-person cost of citizenship.

2. Cost Efficiency That Scales With Family Size

Antigua and Barbuda offers multiple investment routes, but the most popular for large families is the National Development Fund (NDF) contribution:

| Family Size | NDF Contribution (USD) |

|---|---|

| Single applicant | $230,000 |

| Family of 4 or fewer | $230,000 |

| Family of 5+ | $230,000 + $15,000 per additional dependent |

Compare this to other programs:

  • St. Kitts & Nevis: Higher per-dependent fees and a narrower definition of eligible dependents
  • Grenada: Strong program but doesn't include siblings; smaller extended family coverage
  • Malta: Exceptional passport power, but total costs can exceed €1 million for large families
  • Portugal Golden Visa: Investment thresholds are higher, the timeline is longer, and dependent inclusion rules are less flexible

When you calculate the cost per passport for a family of six or seven, Antigua and Barbuda consistently comes out as one of the most affordable CBI programs globally.

3. Additional Investment Routes

Beyond the NDF, families can also qualify through:

  • Real Estate Investment: A minimum investment of $300,000 in a government-approved real estate project (can be held for a minimum of 5 years before resale). Some projects allow joint investments between two applicants.
  • Business Investment: A minimum of $1,500,000 in an approved business venture, or a joint investment of at least $400,000 each by two or more applicants into a single business with a combined value of $5,000,000+.
  • University of the West Indies (UWI) Fund: A contribution of $150,000 for a family of six or more, which also includes a one-year scholarship at UWI for one family member — an excellent option for families with university-aged children.

The UWI Fund option is particularly noteworthy for large families in 2026. It combines the lowest financial threshold with an educational benefit — a genuine two-for-one value proposition.

The Antigua Passport: What Does It Actually Get You?

A citizenship program is only as strong as the passport it delivers. Here's what Antigua and Barbuda's passport offers in 2026:

  • 150+ visa-free or visa-on-arrival destinations
  • UK access without a visa — a critical advantage for families with business, education, or healthcare ties to the United Kingdom
  • Schengen Zone access — travel across 27 European countries with ease
  • Hong Kong and Singapore visa-free entry — key financial hubs in Asia
  • CARICOM freedom of movement — live and work across 15 Caribbean nations

For families who travel frequently, manage international business interests, or want their children educated abroad, this passport opens real doors.

Processing Timeline: Faster Than You'd Expect

In 2026, Antigua and Barbuda's processing timeline is among the most competitive in the Caribbean CBI space:

  • Due diligence and processing: Approximately 3–4 months from submission of a complete application
  • Passport issuance: Typically within 4–6 months total

For a family of six or seven going through a single application, that's remarkably efficient. Compare that to Portugal's Golden Visa, where residency-to-citizenship pathways can stretch 5+ years, or Malta, where the standard naturalization track requires a 12–36 month residency period.

Due Diligence: Rigorous and Getting Stronger

One concern sophisticated families sometimes raise: "Is a Caribbean passport taken seriously?"

The answer in 2026 is a definitive yes — and Antigua's program has earned that credibility through investment in its vetting infrastructure.

The CIU conducts multi-layered due diligence including:

  • International criminal background checks
  • Financial source-of-funds verification
  • Cross-referencing against global sanctions and watchlists
  • In-person interviews where warranted

Antigua and Barbuda has also been an active participant in international transparency initiatives, working with the US, UK, and EU to ensure the integrity of its program. Applicants who pass this process receive a passport that carries genuine international respect.

Real-World Scenarios: Who Benefits Most?

Scenario 1: The Tech Founder With Aging Parents

A 42-year-old SaaS founder based in Dubai wants a Plan B for his family: wife, three children (ages 8, 14, and 22), and his 68-year-old mother. Antigua's program covers all six family members under one application. Total NDF contribution: $230,000 + applicable government and processing fees.

Scenario 2: The Crypto Investor With Siblings

A 35-year-old crypto entrepreneur wants to diversify her citizenship portfolio. She's unmarried but financially supports her younger brother (age 28) and her mother (age 60). Antigua is one of the only programs that allows both as dependents. The UWI Fund route could offer additional value if her brother is pursuing further education.

Scenario 3: The Multi-Generational Business Family

A manufacturing family patriarch (age 62) wants citizenship for himself, his wife, four adult children (ages 24–30), and two of their spouses. While spouses of dependents may require separate applications depending on current rules, Antigua's broad dependent policy still makes it the most efficient single-application solution for the core family unit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do we have to live in Antigua and Barbuda?

No. The only requirement is spending a minimum of 5 days in the country during the first 5 years of citizenship. This can be a family vacation — many families combine it with exactly that.

Is dual citizenship allowed?

Yes. Antigua and Barbuda fully recognizes dual citizenship. You do not need to renounce your existing nationality.

Can my children pass citizenship to their future kids?

Yes. Citizenship acquired through the CBI program is fully heritable — it passes to future generations by descent.

Is there a language or education requirement?

No. There is no language test, civics exam, or interview required for citizenship approval.

What about tax implications?

Antigua and Barbuda has no personal income tax, no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, and no wealth tax. However, applicants should always consult with a tax advisor in their country of residence to understand their specific obligations.

If you're a family-oriented entrepreneur or investor who has been thinking about second citizenship but hasn't taken the first step, the window is open now. Waiting rarely makes these programs cheaper or easier to access.
