Türkiye Citizenship by Investment Through Real Estate: Your Complete 2026 Guide

July 19, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

Why Türkiye Has Become One of the Most Popular CBI Destinations in the World

Türkiye has quietly emerged as one of the most compelling citizenship-by-investment destinations on the global stage — and in 2026, the momentum shows no signs of slowing down.

Straddling two continents, boasting a $1 trillion+ GDP, and offering a passport that provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to over 110 countries, Türkiye delivers something rare in the CBI world: a powerful combination of lifestyle, investment upside, and global mobility at a relatively accessible price point.

For entrepreneurs, investors, and globally mobile professionals looking for a strategic second citizenship, Türkiye deserves a serious look. Here's everything you need to know heading into the second half of 2026.

The Türkiye CBI Program at a Glance

Türkiye first introduced its citizenship-by-investment pathway in 2017, originally setting the real estate threshold at $1 million. In 2018, the government slashed that minimum to $250,000 in a bold move to attract foreign capital. Then, in June 2022, the threshold was raised to $400,000 — where it remains in 2026.

Key Program Requirements (2026):

| Requirement | Details |

|---|---|

| Minimum Investment | $400,000 USD in real estate |

| Holding Period | 3 years (property cannot be sold) |

| Eligible Property Types | Residential, commercial, land, or mixed-use |

| Family Inclusion | Spouse and children under 18 |

| Processing Time | Approximately 3–6 months |

| Citizenship Type | Full citizenship (not residency — actual citizenship) |

| Dual Citizenship | Permitted — no requirement to renounce existing nationality |

Unlike many "golden visa" programs that grant residency with a path to citizenship after 5–10 years, Türkiye offers direct citizenship, which is a significant differentiator in the market.

Why HNW Investors Are Choosing Türkiye in 2026

1. Real Estate With Genuine Upside

This isn't a "park your money and forget about it" program. Türkiye's real estate market has experienced extraordinary growth, particularly in Istanbul, Antalya, Bodrum, and Mersin. According to data from the Turkish Statistical Institute (TurkStat) and global property indices, Turkish real estate has consistently outperformed many European markets in terms of rental yields and capital appreciation — especially when priced in USD.

In 2026, several factors continue to drive investor interest:

  • Infrastructure megaprojects including the Istanbul Canal, new metro lines, and airport expansions
  • Tourism boom — Türkiye welcomed over 60 million tourists in 2024 and numbers continue to climb
  • Urban regeneration programs creating opportunities in emerging neighborhoods
  • Strong rental demand fueled by both domestic migration and international tourism

For investors who think strategically, this isn't just a passport — it's a performing asset.

2. Visa-Free Access to 110+ Destinations

The Turkish passport provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to countries across Asia, Latin America, Africa, and parts of Europe — including Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Qatar, and many others. While it doesn't grant automatic access to the Schengen Zone, Turkish citizens can apply for multi-year Schengen visas with relative ease, and Türkiye's EU accession discussions — while complex — keep the door open for enhanced mobility in the future.

For investors from countries with more restrictive passports, this represents a massive upgrade in global freedom.

3. Gateway to the U.S. E-2 Visa

This is arguably one of Türkiye's most powerful — and underappreciated — advantages.

Türkiye is one of the few CBI-eligible countries that maintains an E-2 Treaty Investor Visa agreement with the United States. This means that once you obtain Turkish citizenship, you can apply for an E-2 visa, which allows you to live and work in the U.S. by investing in or managing a business there.

For entrepreneurs and investors who want a foothold in the American market without going through the EB-5 green card process (which requires $800,000+ and years of waiting), the Türkiye → E-2 pipeline is a game-changer.

4. Speed of Processing

In a world where Portugal's Golden Visa applications face multi-year backlogs and Malta's program involves a 12–36 month residency requirement before citizenship, Türkiye's 3–6 month timeline is exceptionally fast. Some applicants have received their Turkish passport in as little as 90 days.

5. No Residency Requirement

You do not need to live in Türkiye to obtain or maintain your citizenship. There are no minimum stay requirements — not before, during, or after the application. This makes the program ideal for global citizens who want the benefits of a second passport without disrupting their current lifestyle.

The Investment Process: Step by Step

Here's what the journey looks like for a typical applicant in 2026:

Step 1: Initial Consultation & Strategy

Work with an experienced advisory firm (like Meridian) to assess your goals, select the right property type, and understand the tax and legal implications for your specific situation.

Step 2: Property Selection

Identify qualifying properties that meet the $400,000 threshold. Properties can be purchased from Turkish nationals or developers, but purchases between foreign nationals generally do not qualify. An official valuation report is required to confirm the property meets the minimum value.

Step 3: Title Deed (Tapu) Transfer & Annotation

The property is purchased, and a 3-year no-sale annotation is placed on the title deed at the Land Registry Office. This annotation is a formal commitment that you will not sell the property for three years.

Step 4: Certificate of Conformity

The Ministry of Environment, Urbanization, and Climate Change issues a Certificate of Conformity confirming that your investment meets all program requirements.

Step 5: Residency Permit Application

A short-term residency permit is obtained as a procedural step (this does not require you to physically reside in Türkiye).

Step 6: Citizenship Application

Your citizenship application, along with all supporting documents, is submitted to the General Directorate of Civil Registration and Nationality.

Step 7: Passport Issuance

Upon approval, you and your eligible family members receive your Turkish national ID and passport.

What to Watch Out For: Due Diligence Matters

Türkiye's program is legitimate, government-backed, and well-established. However, as with any significant international investment, there are pitfalls that unsophisticated buyers fall into:

  • Overvalued properties: Some developers inflate prices to meet the $400,000 threshold on properties worth significantly less. Always obtain an independent valuation — not one provided by the seller.
  • Title deed issues: Ensure the property has a clean title with no encumbrances, liens, or legal disputes.
  • Currency considerations: The Turkish lira has experienced significant volatility. Properties priced in USD or EUR offer more stability, but understand the currency dynamics before committing.
  • Tax obligations: Understand your tax exposure — both in Türkiye and in your country of current residence or citizenship. Türkiye has double taxation treaties with numerous countries, but professional tax advice is essential.
  • Developer reliability: If purchasing off-plan or under-construction properties, vet the developer's track record thoroughly.

This is exactly why working with an experienced advisory firm matters. The difference between a smooth, successful application and a costly mistake often comes down to the quality of guidance you receive upfront.

Each program has distinct strengths. Türkiye stands out for its combination of direct citizenship, E-2 access, real estate growth potential, and speed — particularly for investors who prioritize tangible assets and a U.S. pathway.

Who Is This Program Best Suited For?

Based on our experience advising clients at Meridian, the Türkiye CBI program is an especially strong fit for:

  • Entrepreneurs seeking U.S. market access via the E-2 visa pipeline
  • Investors from the Middle East, Central Asia, and Africa looking for a significant passport upgrade
  • Crypto holders and digital asset investors looking to diversify into real estate while gaining mobility
  • Business owners who want a Plan B passport quickly without residency disruptions
  • Families who want to create optionality for their children's future — including education and career opportunities across multiple jurisdictions
  • Frequent travelers frustrated by visa restrictions tied to their current passport

What's on the Horizon: Will the Program Change?

CBI programs are inherently political, and thresholds tend to move in one direction: up.

Türkiye has already raised its minimum from $250,000 to $400,000 once before, and there is ongoing discussion about potential further increases — possibly to $500,000 or higher — as the government balances attracting foreign investment with managing domestic housing market dynamics.

There is also speculation about potential changes to the E-2 treaty relationship, though no concrete policy shifts have been announced as of mid-2026.

The takeaway? If Türkiye is on your radar, the current terms represent a known quantity. Waiting introduces the risk of higher thresholds, additional requirements, or program restructuring.
