Why Citizenship by Investment Applications Surge During Election Years and Political Uncertainty

June 08, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

The Pattern Is Unmistakable: Political Uncertainty Drives Demand for Plan B Citizenship

Every election cycle, our phones start ringing earlier. The consultations get longer. The questions get more urgent.

It's not paranoia — it's pattern recognition.

At Meridian Advisory, we've tracked a consistent and well-documented phenomenon: applications for Citizenship by Investment (CBI) programs spike dramatically during periods of political uncertainty, particularly in the months surrounding major elections in the United States, the United Kingdom, and across the European Union.

And 2026 — with midterm elections in the U.S., ongoing geopolitical realignment, and escalating regulatory shifts across multiple jurisdictions — is proving to be no exception.

The Data Tells the Story

The correlation between political volatility and CBI demand isn't anecdotal. It's structural.

  • During the 2016 U.S. presidential election, the St. Kitts & Nevis CBI program reported a 35% year-over-year increase in applications from North American applicants in Q3 and Q4.
  • In 2020, amid a contentious U.S. election, a global pandemic, and widespread social unrest, CBI programs across the Caribbean saw application volumes reach historic highs, with some jurisdictions reporting 40–60% growth.
  • Post-Brexit uncertainty (2016–2020) fueled a sustained surge in Golden Visa applications from UK nationals seeking to retain EU mobility, with Portugal's program seeing record-breaking demand from British citizens.
  • In 2024, a hyper-polarized U.S. presidential election, combined with wars in Ukraine and the Middle East, pushed global CBI inquiries to what industry analysts estimated was an all-time high.
  • Now in 2026, with U.S. midterms approaching, shifting tax policy proposals on the table, and continued geopolitical fragmentation, we're already seeing application volumes trending 20–30% above the same period last year across multiple programs we work with.

The takeaway? Political uncertainty doesn't just make people uncomfortable — it makes them act.

Why Elections Trigger the "Plan B" Instinct

To understand the surge, you need to understand the psychology — and the practicality — behind it.

1. Policy Risk Becomes Personal

Elections don't just determine who holds office. They determine tax policy, immigration law, capital controls, and regulatory frameworks that directly impact high-net-worth individuals.

When a new administration signals potential changes to:

  • Capital gains tax rates
  • Wealth taxes or unrealized gains taxation
  • Crypto regulation and reporting requirements
  • Foreign asset disclosure laws
  • Immigration and visa policies

...the calculus for affluent individuals shifts from "I should look into this someday" to "I need options now."

A second citizenship isn't about leaving. It's about ensuring you're never without choices.

2. Currency and Market Volatility

Election seasons frequently correlate with increased market volatility, currency fluctuations, and investor uncertainty. For entrepreneurs, investors, and anyone with significant liquid assets, a second passport represents a tangible form of geopolitical diversification — a hedge that doesn't expire, doesn't fluctuate with markets, and passes to your children.

3. Travel Freedom Under Threat

Political shifts can — and do — impact visa-free travel agreements. A change in foreign policy direction can result in new visa requirements, strained diplomatic relationships, and reduced mobility for passport holders of affected nations.

Holding citizenship in a jurisdiction like St. Kitts & Nevis (visa-free access to 150+ countries), Grenada (which includes access to the U.S. E-2 Treaty Investor Visa), or an EU member state like Malta provides a structural buffer against these shifts.

4. The "Window" Mentality

Seasoned investors understand that favorable programs don't last forever. Election cycles often bring scrutiny to CBI programs themselves — the EU has already shut down programs in Cyprus and Bulgaria, and ongoing political pressure could reshape others.

This creates urgency: the best time to apply is while the program still exists in its current form.

2026: A Perfect Storm of Uncertainty

Let's look at where things stand right now.

United States

The 2026 midterm elections are shaping up to be a referendum on economic policy, with proposals circulating in Congress around expanded IRS enforcement, new digital asset reporting mandates, and revisions to exit tax provisions. For American entrepreneurs and crypto holders in particular, the regulatory landscape feels like it's shifting under their feet.

Europe

The EU continues to tighten oversight of Golden Visa and residency-by-investment programs. Portugal's program has undergone significant restructuring, and Malta remains one of the last EU citizenship-by-investment pathways — making it both more valuable and more vulnerable to political headwinds.

Global Geopolitics

Ongoing conflicts, supply chain realignment, and the fragmentation of global trade alliances have created an environment where no single passport feels sufficient for the globally mobile individual. Dual citizenship has moved from luxury to strategic necessity.

The Most In-Demand Programs During Uncertain Times

🇰🇳 St. Kitts & Nevis

The gold standard of CBI. Established in 1984, it's the longest-running program in the world. Minimum investment starting at $250,000 in the Sustainable Island State Contribution (SISC). Processing times as fast as 60 days. Visa-free access to 150+ destinations.

Why it surges during uncertainty: Speed, credibility, and decades of proven stability.

🇬🇩 Grenada

The only Caribbean CBI program with access to the U.S. E-2 Treaty Investor Visa, making it uniquely attractive to those who want a pathway to live and work in the United States without a traditional green card.

Why it surges during uncertainty: The E-2 angle is irreplaceable for U.S.-focused entrepreneurs.

🇵🇹 Portugal Golden Visa

Although restructured to focus on investment funds and venture capital (real estate in major cities is no longer eligible), Portugal remains one of the most attractive pathways to EU residency and eventual citizenship. Minimal physical presence requirements and access to the entire Schengen Zone.

Why it surges during uncertainty: EU access is a premium asset that only becomes more valuable as mobility tightens elsewhere.

🇲🇹 Malta

The only current EU citizenship-by-investment program, Malta offers a direct path to an EU passport — arguably the most powerful travel document in the world. Higher investment thresholds (typically €700,000+), but the value proposition is unmatched for those who qualify.

Why it surges during uncertainty: Scarcity. There's no other way to invest directly into EU citizenship, and the program faces ongoing political scrutiny that could alter or close it.

The Cost of Waiting

Here's what we tell every client who says "I'll look into this after the election":

CBI programs change. They get restructured. They get more expensive. Some get shut down entirely.

  • Cyprus closed its program in 2020.
  • Bulgaria ended its Golden Visa in 2022.
  • Portugal removed Lisbon and Porto real estate from its program in 2023.
  • Minimum investment thresholds across Caribbean programs have increased multiple times in the last five years.

The trend line is clear: programs get harder to access over time, not easier.

Processing times also matter. Most CBI programs take 60 to 180 days from application to approval. If you're reacting to an election result rather than preparing in advance, you've already lost months.
