Portugal Golden Visa Through Investment Funds: Your Complete 2026 Guide

May 31, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

By Meridian Advisory | Updated June 2026

The Portugal Golden Visa Is Alive and Well — Just Not Through Real Estate

If you've been following European residency-by-investment news, you've likely heard the noise: "Portugal killed the Golden Visa."

That's not quite right.

What Portugal did — back in October 2023 — was eliminate the real estate investment route. What it very much kept intact is one of the most compelling pathways to European residency available today: investment fund subscriptions.

And in 2026, this route is not just surviving. It's thriving.

How the Portugal Golden Visa Works in 2026

The Golden Visa (Autorização de Residência para Atividade de Investimento, or ARI) grants non-EU nationals a Portuguese residency permit in exchange for a qualifying investment. That residency permit comes with extraordinary benefits:

  • Visa-free access to the entire Schengen Area (29 European countries)
  • Minimal physical presence requirement — just 7 days per year on average
  • Path to permanent residency after 5 years
  • Path to Portuguese citizenship after 5 years (one of the shortest timelines in Europe)
  • Access to Portugal's Non-Habitual Resident (NHR 2.0) tax regime for qualifying applicants
  • Family inclusion — spouse, dependent children, and even dependent parents can be added

The critical question in 2026 isn't whether the Golden Visa is worth pursuing. It's how you structure your qualifying investment for maximum benefit.

The Investment Fund Route: What Qualifies

Under current Portuguese law, the qualifying investment fund route requires:

Minimum investment: €500,000 into a qualifying Portuguese investment fund.

To qualify, the fund must meet the following criteria:

  • ✅ Registered and regulated by the CMVM (Comissão do Mercado de Valores Mobiliários — Portugal's securities regulator)
  • ✅ Maturity of at least 5 years (matching the residency timeline)
  • ✅ At least 60% of the fund's portfolio must be invested in Portuguese-based companies or assets
  • ✅ The fund must not be concentrated in real estate (funds that primarily invest in property are excluded under the 2023 amendments)
  • ✅ The fund must qualify as a venture capital fund, private equity fund, or similar vehicle under Portuguese regulations

This is not a donation. This is not dead capital. Your €500,000 is an investment with a potential return, and after the fund's maturity period, you get your capital back — ideally with gains.

Why Investment Funds Have Become the Dominant Golden Visa Pathway

The numbers tell the story. Since real estate was removed as a qualifying category, investment fund applications have surged to represent the overwhelming majority of new Golden Visa approvals in 2025 and into 2026.

Here's why sophisticated investors are gravitating to this route:

1. Genuine Investment Returns

Unlike citizenship-by-donation programs (where your capital is gone), qualified Portuguese funds invest in real economic activity — technology startups, renewable energy, life sciences, established Portuguese enterprises. Historical returns vary, but well-managed funds have targeted annual returns in the 3–8% range, depending on the strategy and risk profile.

2. European Diversification

For investors with portfolios heavily concentrated in North America, Asia, or the Middle East, a Portuguese fund allocation provides genuine geographic diversification into the European market.

3. Five-Year Alignment

The mandatory fund lock-up period of 5+ years aligns perfectly with the Golden Visa's residency timeline. By the time you're eligible for permanent residency or citizenship, your investment has also matured.

4. Professional Management

These are regulated, professionally managed vehicles — not speculative plays. CMVM oversight provides a layer of institutional credibility and investor protection that many other CBI/RBI programs simply can't match.

The Path to Portuguese (and EU) Citizenship

This is where the Portugal Golden Visa truly separates itself from nearly every other program globally. Let's map the timeline:

| Milestone | Timeline |

|---|---|

| Investment made & application submitted | Month 0 |

| Biometrics appointment in Portugal | ~2–4 months |

| Golden Visa residency card issued | ~8–16 months |

| First renewal | Year 2 |

| Second renewal | Year 4 |

| Eligible for permanent residency | Year 5 |

| Eligible for Portuguese citizenship | Year 5+ |

Portuguese citizenship = EU citizenship. That means the right to live, work, study, and do business in any of the 27 EU member states — Germany, France, Netherlands, Ireland, all of them — plus access to 180+ countries visa-free on a Portuguese passport.

And here's the part that makes global planners pay attention: Portugal allows dual citizenship. You don't have to give up your existing passport.

The Physical Presence "Requirement"

The Golden Visa is famous for having one of the lightest residency obligations of any program worldwide:

  • Year 1: 7 days
  • Years 2–3: 14 days total
  • Years 4–5: 14 days total

That's a total of approximately 35 days over 5 years. You can maintain your life, your business, and your primary residence wherever you are today — and still qualify for EU citizenship.

Note: For citizenship (as opposed to just permanent residency), applicants will need to demonstrate ties to Portugal and pass a basic Portuguese language test (A2 level). We recommend beginning language preparation early in the process.

How to Choose the Right Fund

This is the decision that matters most — and where many applicants make mistakes. Not all CMVM-registered funds are created equal. Here's what to evaluate:

Fund Manager Track Record

How long has the management company been operating? Do they have prior fund performance data? Have they managed Golden Visa-specific funds before, and do they understand the regulatory intersection of immigration and securities law?

Investment Strategy

What sectors does the fund target? Common strategies include:

  • Venture Capital — early-stage Portuguese startups (higher risk, higher potential return)
  • Private Equity — growth and mid-market Portuguese companies (moderate risk)
  • Balanced/Diversified — multi-sector portfolios with a mix of equity and debt instruments

Fee Structure

Management fees, performance fees, entry fees — these vary widely and directly impact your net returns. Transparency here is non-negotiable.

Immigration Compliance

The fund must remain compliant with Golden Visa requirements for the entire 5-year period. If a fund loses its qualifying status, your residency could be jeopardized. This is why working with an experienced advisor who conducts independent due diligence is critical.

Exit Terms

What happens at maturity? What are the liquidity options? Is there a secondary market for fund units? Understand your exit before you enter.

Common Misconceptions in 2026

Let's clear up the misinformation we see circulating:

❌ "The Portugal Golden Visa program is closed."

No. The real estate route was closed. The investment fund, capital transfer, research, and job creation routes remain fully operational.

❌ "You need to live in Portugal."

No. The minimum stay is approximately 7 days per year. This is a residency program you can hold while living anywhere in the world.

❌ "The €500,000 is a sunk cost."

No. This is an investment into a regulated fund with a return profile. You are an investor, not a donor.

❌ "Processing takes years."

Processing times have improved considerably from their pandemic-era backlog. While timelines vary, many applicants in 2026 are seeing initial approvals in under 12 months.

❌ "Any fund will do."

Absolutely not. Fund selection is the single most important decision in this process. The wrong fund can mean poor returns, compliance issues, or even jeopardizing your residency status.

Who Is This Program Best Suited For?

The Portugal Golden Visa through investment funds is particularly compelling for:

  • 🌍 Entrepreneurs and business owners seeking a Plan B in a stable EU jurisdiction
  • 💼 Investors looking for portfolio diversification with an immigration benefit attached
  • 🪙 Crypto holders and digital asset investors planning for regulatory clarity and global optionality
  • ✈️ Frequent international travelers who want Schengen-wide access without visa hassles
  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Families planning for children's future education and career opportunities in Europe
  • 🏦 Tax planners exploring Portugal's NHR 2.0 regime in conjunction with residency
