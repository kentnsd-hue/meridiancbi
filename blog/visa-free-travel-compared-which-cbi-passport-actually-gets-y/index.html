Visa-Free Travel Compared: Which CBI Passport Actually Gets You Where in 2026?

April 28, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Not all second passports are created equal. Here's a data-driven breakdown of where each Citizenship by Investment passport can take you — and which one aligns with your travel, business, and lifestyle goals.

Why Visa-Free Access Is the Real Currency of a Second Passport

When high-net-worth individuals explore Citizenship by Investment, the conversation almost always starts with the same question:

"Where can I actually go with this passport?"

It's the right question. Visa-free travel access is one of the most tangible, immediate benefits of acquiring a second citizenship. It determines whether you can fly to London for a last-minute deal, attend a conference in Singapore without a three-week visa application, or move freely across the European Union.

But here's where most people get it wrong: they fixate on the raw number of visa-free destinations without examining which destinations matter for their specific life and business needs.

Let's fix that.

The 2026 Visa-Free Scorecard: Program by Program

Below is a comprehensive comparison of the most popular CBI programs, ranked by visa-free and visa-on-arrival access as of early 2026.

🇲🇹 Malta

Visa-Free / Visa-on-Arrival Destinations: ~190

Malta sits at the top of the CBI hierarchy for one simple reason: it's an EU member state. A Maltese passport is, functionally, a European Union passport.

Where it gets you:

  • 🇪🇺 Full freedom of movement across all 27 EU member states (live, work, study — no restrictions)
  • 🇺🇸 United States (ESTA visa waiver)
  • 🇨🇦 Canada (eTA)
  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom (visa-free for up to 6 months)
  • 🇯🇵 Japan, 🇸🇬 Singapore, 🇦🇺 Australia (ETA), 🇳🇿 New Zealand
  • Virtually all of South America, the Caribbean, and Africa

Best for: Individuals who need unlimited EU access — whether for business operations, family relocation, or long-term European residency. Also ideal for those who frequently travel to the US and want frictionless entry.

Investment minimum: ~€690,000+ (including real estate, government contribution, and fees for the Maltese Exceptional Investor Naturalisation programme). Processing typically takes 12–14 months after a residency period.

The trade-off: Highest cost. Most rigorous due diligence. Longest timeline. But pound for pound, the strongest passport money can buy through a CBI channel.

🇵🇹 Portugal Golden Visa (Path to Citizenship)

Visa-Free / Visa-on-Arrival Destinations: ~190

Portugal's Golden Visa isn't technically a CBI program — it's a residency-by-investment program with a pathway to citizenship after five years. But because Portuguese citizenship delivers an EU passport with virtually identical mobility to Malta, it deserves a place in this comparison.

Where it gets you:

  • 🇪🇺 Full EU freedom of movement
  • 🇺🇸 United States (ESTA)
  • 🇨🇦 Canada (eTA)
  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom
  • 🇯🇵 Japan, 🇰🇷 South Korea, 🇸🇬 Singapore, 🇦🇺 Australia

Best for: Those who can plan on a 5+ year timeline and want one of the most powerful passports in the world at a lower entry point than Malta. Portugal's program also allows minimal physical presence requirements — you only need to spend an average of 7 days per year in Portugal during the residency phase.

Investment minimum: Starting from €500,000 (fund-based investments, as direct real estate purchases in major cities were restructured in 2023).

The trade-off: You're not getting a passport next year. This is a long game — residency first, citizenship after ~5 years, contingent on passing a basic Portuguese language test. But the end result is one of the top 5 passports on earth.

🇬🇩 Grenada

Visa-Free / Visa-on-Arrival Destinations: ~148

Grenada is the quiet powerhouse of Caribbean CBI programs, and it holds one critical advantage that no other Caribbean passport offers.

Where it gets you:

  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom (visa-free, 6 months)
  • 🇪🇺 Schengen Area (visa-free, 90 days in any 180-day period)
  • 🇸🇬 Singapore
  • 🇨🇳 China (visa-free since 2024 — a major differentiator)
  • 🇷🇺 Russia
  • 🇧🇷 Brazil, and most of South America and the Caribbean
  • 🇭🇰 Hong Kong

The Grenada Ace Card — E-2 Treaty Access to the US:

Grenada is the only Caribbean CBI country with an E-2 Investor Visa treaty with the United States. This means Grenadian citizens can apply for a US E-2 visa, allowing them to live and work in the US by investing in or operating a US-based business. This is not a tourist visa — it's a renewable, long-term residency pathway.

For anyone who wants a foothold in the American market but doesn't qualify for an EB-5 or doesn't want to wait years in a green card backlog, the Grenada + E-2 combination is arguably the most strategic play available in 2026.

Best for: Entrepreneurs who want Caribbean tax advantages plus a pathway into the US. Also excellent for those doing business in China, the UK, and the EU.

Investment minimum: $235,000 donation to the National Transformation Fund, or $270,000+ in approved real estate.

The trade-off: No direct US visa-free entry (you still need the E-2 visa, which requires a separate application and a qualifying US business investment). No access to Japan, South Korea, or Australia without a visa.

🇰🇳 St. Kitts & Nevis

Visa-Free / Visa-on-Arrival Destinations: ~157

The original CBI program — established in 1984 — and still one of the most trusted and efficient. St. Kitts has the longest track record and strong global recognition.

Where it gets you:

  • 🇬🇧 United Kingdom (visa-free, 6 months)
  • 🇪🇺 Schengen Area (visa-free, 90 days)
  • 🇸🇬 Singapore
  • 🇭🇰 Hong Kong
  • 🇷🇺 Russia
  • 🇲🇾 Malaysia, 🇵🇭 Philippines, 🇧🇩 Bangladesh
  • Most of Africa, South America, and the Caribbean

Best for: Speed and reliability. St. Kitts is known for processing applications in as little as 45–60 days under its Accelerated Application Process (AAP). If you need a second passport fast with strong travel coverage across Europe, the UK, and Asia-Pacific, St. Kitts is the gold standard of Caribbean programs.

Investment minimum: $250,000 donation to the Sustainable Island State Contribution (SISC), or $325,000+ in approved real estate.

The trade-off: No E-2 treaty with the US (unlike Grenada). No visa-free access to China, Japan, South Korea, or Australia.

So, Which Passport Should You Choose?

The honest answer: it depends entirely on where you need to go and why.

Here's a simplified decision framework:

Choose Malta or Portugal if:

  • You need to live, work, or operate a business inside the European Union
  • You travel frequently to the US and Japan and need seamless entry
  • You want the highest-tier passport with maximum global recognition
  • You can invest €500K+ and are comfortable with a longer timeline

Choose Grenada if:

  • You want a US market entry strategy via the E-2 visa
  • You do business in or travel frequently to China
  • You're looking for a Caribbean tax-neutral jurisdiction
  • You want citizenship in under 6 months at a lower price point

Choose St. Kitts & Nevis if:

  • Speed is your top priority — you need a second passport in weeks, not months or years
  • Your primary travel corridors are Europe, the UK, and Southeast Asia
  • You want the longest-established, most proven CBI program
  • You value simplicity and a streamlined process

What Most People Miss: It's Not Just About the Passport

Visa-free destination counts change. Treaties get signed (and revoked). Governments update their policies.

What doesn't change is the strategic value of having optionality. A second citizenship is not just a travel document — it's a hedge against political instability, a tool for tax planning, an insurance policy for your family, and a gateway to international banking, business incorporation, and generational wealth protection.

The best CBI decision isn't the one with the highest number next to it. It's the one that's been architected around your specific life — your business operations, your family's needs, your travel patterns, and your long-term financial strategy.
