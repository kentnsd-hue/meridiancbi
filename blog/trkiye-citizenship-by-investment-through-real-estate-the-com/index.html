Türkiye Citizenship by Investment Through Real Estate: The Complete 2026 Guide

May 27, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

Why Türkiye Has Become One of the Most Popular CBI Destinations in the World

Türkiye has quietly emerged as one of the most compelling citizenship-by-investment destinations on the global stage. Straddling Europe and Asia, boasting a $1 trillion+ GDP, and offering a lifestyle that rivals countries charging three times the investment threshold, Türkiye's real estate pathway to citizenship has attracted tens of thousands of foreign investors over the past several years.

In 2026, the program remains one of the most accessible and strategically valuable routes to a second passport — and demand shows no signs of slowing down.

Here's everything you need to know.

The Basics: How Türkiye's Real Estate CBI Program Works

Türkiye's citizenship-by-investment program allows foreign nationals to obtain full Turkish citizenship — including a passport — by purchasing real estate valued at a minimum of $400,000 USD.

Key Program Requirements:

| Criteria | Details |

|---|---|

| Minimum Investment | $400,000 USD in real estate |

| Holding Period | 3 years (property cannot be sold before this period ends) |

| Property Type | Residential, commercial, or land |

| Number of Properties | One or multiple, as long as total value meets the threshold |

| Family Inclusion | Spouse and children under 18 are included |

| Residency Requirement | None — you do not need to live in Türkiye |

| Processing Time | Approximately 3–6 months |

| Dual Citizenship | Fully permitted — no need to renounce existing citizenship |

The $400,000 threshold has been in place since June 2022, when it was raised from $250,000. As of mid-2026, there has been no official announcement of a further increase, but given the trajectory of global CBI programs tightening requirements, locking in at the current threshold is a strategic move.

Why Investors Are Choosing Türkiye in 2026

1. One of the Lowest Entry Points for Full Citizenship

At $400,000, Türkiye offers one of the most affordable pathways to a full citizenship and passport — not just residency. Compare that to:

  • Malta: €690,000+ (plus additional fees and contributions exceeding €1M total)
  • Portugal Golden Visa: €500,000+ (and now limited to specific fund investments — direct real estate purchases in major cities are largely restricted)
  • Caribbean Programs (St. Kitts, Grenada): $200,000–$250,000 in donations, but these are non-recoverable costs with no asset ownership

Türkiye stands out because your $400,000 goes into a tangible, appreciating asset that you own, can rent out, and can sell after three years.

2. A Genuinely Powerful Passport

The Turkish passport provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 110+ countries and territories as of 2026, including:

  • Japan
  • South Korea
  • Singapore
  • All of South America
  • Much of Central Asia and the Middle East

While it doesn't offer visa-free access to the EU's Schengen Area, Türkiye's ongoing diplomatic negotiations and its status as an EU candidate country could shift this landscape over time.

More importantly for many investors, the Turkish passport serves as a gateway to the United States through the E-2 Treaty Investor Visa — a benefit we'll cover in detail below.

3. The U.S. E-2 Visa Advantage

This is arguably Türkiye's most underrated strategic benefit in 2026.

Türkiye is one of the few CBI-eligible countries that maintains an E-2 Treaty of Commerce and Navigation with the United States. This means Turkish citizens can apply for the E-2 Investor Visa, which allows them to:

  • Live and work in the United States
  • Bring their spouse and dependents
  • Renew the visa indefinitely (typically in 5-year increments)

For investors from countries that do not have an E-2 treaty with the U.S. — including China, India, the UAE, South Africa, and most of the Middle East — obtaining Turkish citizenship first creates a legitimate, legal pathway to living in America without the EB-5 backlog, which in 2026 continues to stretch years for applicants from high-demand countries.

This single benefit has made Türkiye's CBI program enormously popular among investors from the Gulf states and East Asia.

4. Booming Real Estate Market

Türkiye's real estate market has shown remarkable resilience and growth. Key trends in 2026:

  • Istanbul remains the primary investment hub, with neighborhoods like Başakşehir, Beylikdüzü, and Kadıköy showing strong rental yields and capital appreciation
  • Antalya and the Mediterranean coast continue to attract lifestyle investors and short-term rental operators
  • Ankara and Izmir offer emerging opportunities at lower price points with institutional-grade developments
  • Infrastructure mega-projects — including Istanbul's continued airport expansion, new metro lines, and Canal Istanbul developments — are driving property values in surrounding areas

Rental yields in prime Istanbul locations are averaging 5–7% annually in USD-equivalent terms, providing a genuine income stream alongside capital growth.

5. No Residency Obligation

Unlike many residency and citizenship programs, Türkiye does not require you to live in the country at any point during the process. You can:

  • Apply from abroad
  • Receive your citizenship without relocating
  • Use your Turkish passport purely for travel, banking, or business purposes

This makes it ideal for busy entrepreneurs, digital nomads, and investors who want optionality without disruption.

The Step-by-Step Process in 2026

Here's what the typical journey looks like:

Step 1: Consultation & Strategy (Week 1)

Work with an experienced advisory firm (like Meridian Advisory) to determine if Türkiye is the right fit for your goals — or whether another program better serves your needs.

Step 2: Property Selection & Due Diligence (Weeks 2–4)

Identify qualifying properties, verify clean title deeds, and ensure the property appraisal meets or exceeds the $400,000 threshold. This step is critical — incorrect valuations or encumbered titles can derail applications.

Step 3: Purchase & Title Deed Transfer (Weeks 4–6)

Complete the property purchase, obtain your TAPU (Turkish title deed), and secure the official annotation confirming the property cannot be sold for three years.

Step 4: Citizenship Application Submission (Weeks 6–8)

Submit your application with all supporting documents, including proof of investment, background checks, and family documentation.

Step 5: Residence Permit Issuance (Weeks 8–12)

Receive a Turkish residence permit while your citizenship application is being processed.

Step 6: Citizenship Approval & Passport (Months 3–6)

Upon approval, receive your Turkish national ID and apply for your Turkish passport.

Total timeline from start to passport in hand: approximately 3–6 months, making it one of the fastest CBI programs in the world.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Having advised clients across dozens of CBI programs, we see the same pitfalls repeatedly with Türkiye applications:

❌ Overpaying for undervalued properties

Some developers inflate prices specifically for CBI buyers, meaning you pay $400,000 for a property genuinely worth $280,000. Always get an independent valuation.

❌ Skipping legal due diligence

Ensure the title is clean, the property is free of liens, and the developer has all necessary permits. Work with an independent Turkish lawyer — not one provided by the seller.

❌ Ignoring currency dynamics

The Turkish lira has experienced significant volatility. Real estate transactions for CBI purposes are typically priced in USD or EUR, but understanding the local currency dynamics matters for rental income and resale planning.

❌ Not considering exit strategy

You must hold for three years. After that, what's your plan? Will you sell, continue renting, or hold long-term? Having a clear exit strategy before you buy is essential.

❌ Going through unvetted agents

The Türkiye CBI space has attracted numerous unregulated intermediaries. Always work with established advisory firms with verifiable track records.

Who Is Türkiye's CBI Program Ideal For?

Based on our experience at Meridian Advisory, the investors who benefit most from Turkish citizenship include:

  • Entrepreneurs from non-E-2 treaty countries who want a pathway to the U.S.
  • Gulf-based investors and business owners seeking geographic diversification
  • Crypto and digital asset holders looking for a tax-efficient second jurisdiction
  • Frequent international travelers who need expanded visa-free access
  • Families seeking a Plan B with the option to relocate to a stable, modern country
  • Real estate investors who want their CBI investment to generate actual returns

What's on the Horizon for 2026 and Beyond?

Several developments are worth watching:

1. Potential threshold increase: Multiple industry sources suggest Türkiye may raise the minimum investment to $500,000 or higher. No official timeline has been confirmed, but the pattern across global CBI programs is clear — prices go up, not down.

2. Enhanced due diligence: Türkiye has been strengthening its vetting processes in line with international standards, which is ultimately positive for the program's long-term credibility.

3. EU accession progress: While full EU membership remains a long-term prospect, any movement in Türkiye-EU relations could significantly enhance the Turkish passport's value.

4. Growing institutional real estate: International developers are increasingly building CBI-qualifying projects with professional management, making the investment component more attractive and reliable.

The Bottom Line

Türkiye's citizenship-by-investment program through real estate remains one of the smartest moves available to global investors in 2026. The combination of genuine asset ownership, a powerful passport, U.S. E-2 access, fast processing, and no residency requirement creates a package that very few programs can match.

But like any significant financial and legal decision, the details matter enormously. The wrong property, the wrong advisor, or the wrong structure can turn a strategic opportunity into an expensive mistake.
