CBI Processing Times Compared: How Long Does It Really Take to Get a Second Passport in 2026?

May 04, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

By Meridian Advisory | Updated June 2026

One of the first questions we hear from clients is deceptively simple: "How long will this take?"

It's the right question. Whether you're planning an international expansion, restructuring your tax residency, or simply building a contingency plan for your family, timing matters. A second passport isn't useful if it arrives six months after you needed it.

The problem? Processing times vary wildly — from as little as 60 days to well over a year — depending on the program, your personal circumstances, and how well your application is prepared.

We've compiled the most current processing data across the world's leading Citizenship by Investment programs so you can plan accordingly.

Tier 1: The Fast Track (Under 90 Days)

🇰🇳 St. Kitts & Nevis — The Gold Standard of Speed

St. Kitts operates the oldest CBI program in the world (established 1984), and in 2026, it remains one of the fastest. The Accelerated Application Option (AAO) can deliver citizenship in as little as 45 days for an additional government fee.

Why it's fast: The Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU) in St. Kitts has invested heavily in digital processing infrastructure and maintains one of the most experienced due diligence teams in the Caribbean. Their four-decade track record means fewer bottlenecks.

Best for: Clients who need a reliable, respected passport quickly — particularly those with upcoming travel plans, business deadlines, or geopolitical urgency.

What can slow it down: Incomplete documentation, complex corporate structures, or applicants from higher-risk jurisdictions that require enhanced due diligence.

🇻🇺 Vanuatu — The Fastest in the World

Vanuatu consistently delivers the shortest processing times of any CBI program globally, with some applications approved in as few as 30 days. The Development Support Program (DSP) is streamlined and efficient.

Why it's fast: Vanuatu's program is lean by design — a smaller applicant pool, a centralized review process, and a government that has prioritized CBI as a key revenue stream.

The trade-off: While the speed is unmatched, a Vanuatu passport offers visa-free access to roughly 90+ destinations — strong, but not on par with Caribbean or European programs. It's also drawn increased scrutiny from international regulatory bodies, so prospective applicants should weigh reputational considerations.

Best for: Clients who prioritize speed above all else and need a functional travel document or "Plan B" passport quickly.

Tier 2: The Sweet Spot (3–6 Months)

🇬🇩 Grenada — Speed Meets Strategic Value

Grenada typically processes applications in 4 to 6 months, though well-prepared applications can clear in closer to 90 days. What makes Grenada stand out isn't just speed — it's what the passport unlocks.

The E-2 advantage: Grenada is one of the only CBI countries with a Treaty of Commerce and Navigation with the United States. This means Grenadian citizens can apply for the U.S. E-2 Investor Visa, allowing them to live and work in the U.S. by investing in a U.S.-based business. For clients who want a pathway into the American market without the complexity (and decade-long wait) of the EB-5 program, this is a game-changer.

Why we recommend it: At Meridian Advisory, Grenada is one of our most-recommended programs because of this unique combination of reasonable processing time, competitive pricing, and strategic U.S. access.

Best for: Entrepreneurs eyeing the U.S. market, families seeking strong visa-free travel (140+ destinations), and investors who value long-term strategic optionality.

🇦🇬 Antigua & Barbuda — Family-Friendly and Efficient

Antigua processes most applications within 3 to 6 months and is particularly attractive for larger families. The program allows inclusion of dependent children up to age 30 and dependent parents over 55, making it one of the most generous in terms of family eligibility.

Best for: Families. If you have adult children in university or aging parents you'd like to include in your citizenship plan, Antigua offers flexibility that many other programs don't.

🇩🇲 Dominica — The Budget-Conscious Choice

Dominica's program, with a donation starting at $200,000 for a single applicant, remains the most affordable CBI option in the Caribbean. Processing times sit in the 3 to 6 month range.

The trade-off: Dominica has been under increased international pressure to tighten its due diligence — which is ultimately a good thing for the program's long-term credibility, but it has added modest delays compared to a few years ago.

Best for: Solo applicants or couples who are cost-conscious and don't require the E-2 treaty access that Grenada offers.

🇹🇷 Turkey — Real Estate and Residency in One

Turkey's program requires a minimum $400,000 real estate investment and processes in 3 to 6 months. A Turkish passport provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 110+ destinations and serves as a strong base for business in Europe, the Middle East, and Central Asia.

Best for: Investors who want a tangible real estate asset combined with citizenship, particularly those doing business across the MENA region.

Tier 3: The Long Game (12+ Months)

🇵🇹 Portugal Golden Visa — Europe's Most Coveted Residency Path

Let's be direct: the Portugal Golden Visa is not fast. Current processing times stretch to 12 to 24 months or more, and the program has undergone significant restructuring. Real estate investment is no longer a qualifying route — the primary option in 2026 is a €500,000 investment into qualifying Portuguese funds.

Why people wait: Because the end result — permanent residency and eventually citizenship in an EU member state — is extraordinarily valuable. A Portuguese passport is one of the most powerful in the world, offering visa-free access to 180+ countries, full EU residency rights, and access to Europe's Schengen Area.

The residency requirement: Portugal requires minimal physical presence (approximately 7 days per year on average), making it one of the few European programs compatible with a globally mobile lifestyle.

The citizenship timeline: After 5 years of legal residency, you can apply for Portuguese citizenship. So the total timeline from initial application to EU passport is realistically 6 to 8 years.

Best for: Clients who are playing the long game — building a European foothold for themselves, their families, and their businesses. This is a generational asset.

🇲🇹 Malta — The Premium European Option

Malta's Exceptional Investor Naturalization (MEIN) program is the only legitimate direct citizenship-by-investment program in the European Union. It's also the most expensive and the slowest.

The timeline: A minimum 12-month residency requirement (36 months for the lower investment tier) before citizenship is granted. Combined with processing, you're looking at 1 to 3 years total.

The investment: A composite investment of approximately €690,000 to €890,000+, including a government contribution, real estate (purchase or lease), and a philanthropic donation.

Why it's worth it: A Maltese passport is an EU passport. Full stop. It grants unrestricted right to live, work, and do business anywhere in the European Union. It offers visa-free access to 180+ destinations, including the United States. For clients who want the absolute strongest travel document combined with EU membership, Malta is the apex.

Best for: Ultra-high-net-worth individuals and families who want the best passport money can buy and are willing to invest the time and capital to get it.

What Actually Affects Your Processing Time?

The timelines above are averages. In our experience at Meridian Advisory, the actual speed of your application depends on five key factors:

1. Quality of Documentation

This is the single biggest variable — and the one you have the most control over. Incomplete applications get sent back. Missing apostilles, untranslated documents, or inconsistencies in financial records can add weeks or months. Working with an experienced advisory firm dramatically reduces this risk.

2. Source of Funds Verification

Every legitimate CBI program conducts thorough due diligence on where your investment capital comes from. If your wealth comes from straightforward sources (salary, business sale, inheritance with clear documentation), this moves quickly. Complex multi-jurisdictional corporate structures or cryptocurrency-derived wealth require additional verification time.

3. Your Nationality and Background

Applicants from certain countries face enhanced due diligence — not as a judgment, but as a regulatory requirement. This can add 30 to 90 days to processing. An experienced advisor knows which programs process which nationalities most efficiently and can guide your strategy accordingly.

4. Family Size and Complexity

Adding dependents means additional background checks for each family member. A family of six will naturally take longer than a solo applicant.

5. Program Backlogs and Policy Changes

CBI programs are run by sovereign governments, and they're subject to political, economic, and regulatory shifts. A sudden surge in applications, a change in government, or new international compliance requirements can create temporary delays. Staying current on these dynamics is part of what we do at Meridian Advisory.

A Word on "Guaranteed" Timelines

If anyone guarantees you a specific passport delivery date, be cautious. No advisory firm controls sovereign government processing. What a good advisory firm does is:

  • Prepare your application to the highest possible standard to avoid delays
  • Maintain direct relationships with government CBI units
  • Flag potential issues before they become problems
  • Keep you informed at every stage with realistic expectations

So Which Program Is Right for You?

The "best" CBI program doesn't exist in a vacuum. It depends on your specific situation:

  • Need a passport in 60 days? → St. Kitts & Nevis (Accelerated) or Vanuatu
  • Want U.S. market access? → Grenada (E-2 treaty)
  • Building a European future? → Portugal Golden Visa or Malta
  • Optimizing for budget? → Dominica
  • Large family? → Antigua & Barbuda
  • Want real estate + citizenship? → Turkey
  • Want the strongest passport possible? → Malta
