CBI Processing Times Compared: How Long Does It Really Take to Get a Second Passport in 2026?

June 08, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | June 2026

One of the first questions every prospective applicant asks is deceptively simple: "How long will this take?"

It's also one of the most misunderstood aspects of citizenship by investment. Marketing brochures promise one timeline. Reality often delivers another. Government backlogs, due diligence bottlenecks, and shifting policy landscapes all play a role.

At Meridian Advisory, we believe in setting accurate expectations from day one. So let's break down the real processing times across the most popular CBI and residency-by-investment programs in 2026 — and what actually drives those timelines behind the scenes.

The Quick Reference: Processing Times at a Glance

|---|---|---|---|

| St. Kitts & Nevis | 45–60 days (Accelerated) | 60–90 days | $250,000 (donation) |

| Grenada | 90–120 days | 4–6 months | $235,000 (donation) |

| Turkey | 3–6 months | 6–9 months | $400,000 (real estate) |

Note: Timelines reflect application-to-passport for CBI programs and application-to-residency-card for residency programs, unless otherwise stated. All figures current as of mid-2026.

Program-by-Program Deep Dive

🇰🇳 St. Kitts & Nevis: The Gold Standard in Speed

St. Kitts runs the oldest CBI program in the world (established 1984), and it shows in their operational efficiency. The Accelerated Application Process (AAP) is designed for applicants who need urgency and are willing to pay a premium for it.

What drives the timeline:

  • The Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU) has invested heavily in digitizing due diligence workflows in recent years
  • AAP applications are prioritized in the review queue
  • Standard (non-accelerated) applications can take 3–4 months

Best for: Applicants who need a passport quickly — whether for an imminent business deal, travel requirement, or geopolitical contingency planning.

The catch: The accelerated processing fee adds to total costs. But for many clients, the speed justifies the premium.

🇬🇩 Grenada: The Strategic Choice (With a Bit More Patience)

Grenada consistently ranks as one of the most strategically valuable CBI passports, primarily because it's one of the few Caribbean programs whose citizens qualify for the U.S. E-2 Treaty Investor Visa. That single advantage makes it the program of choice for entrepreneurs who want a pathway into the American market.

What drives the timeline:

  • Grenada's due diligence process is thorough, involving multiple international verification agencies
  • Application volume has increased significantly since 2023, which has created modest backlogs
  • The government has been expanding processing capacity, but demand continues to outpace infrastructure

Realistic expectation in 2026: Budget 4–6 months from submission of a complete application to passport in hand.

Best for: Entrepreneurs targeting the U.S. market, families seeking a well-regarded Caribbean passport with strong visa-free travel.

🇩🇲 Dominica: The Quiet Overperformer

Dominica doesn't generate the same headlines as St. Kitts or Grenada, but it consistently delivers one of the best value propositions in the CBI world. It's the most affordable program with reliable processing times.

What drives the timeline:

  • Dominica's program is well-run with a streamlined internal process
  • Lower application volume compared to St. Kitts means fewer bottlenecks
  • The government has been disciplined about maintaining due diligence standards without unnecessary delays

Best for: Cost-conscious applicants who want a solid Caribbean passport without paying a premium for brand recognition.

🇻🇺 Vanuatu: The Fastest Passport on Earth

If raw speed is your primary criterion, Vanuatu is virtually unmatched. Some applicants have reported receiving citizenship approval in as little as 30 days.

What drives the timeline:

  • Vanuatu's Development Support Program (DSP) was explicitly designed for rapid processing
  • The country's smaller bureaucracy means fewer layers of approval
  • Due diligence is conducted but is less extensive than Caribbean or European programs

The important caveat: Speed comes with trade-offs. Vanuatu's passport offers visa-free access to approximately 100 destinations — solid, but meaningfully less than St. Kitts (~160) or Grenada (~145). Additionally, Vanuatu CBI has faced periodic scrutiny from international bodies, which can affect long-term passport strength.

Best for: Applicants who need the fastest possible second nationality and are comfortable with a more limited travel profile.

🇲🇹 Malta: The European Gold Standard

Malta's Exceptional Investor Naturalization (MEIN) program is in a league of its own. It's the only program that grants full EU citizenship through investment, which means unrestricted rights to live, work, and do business across all 27 EU member states.

That privilege comes with a rigorous process.

What drives the timeline:

  • Malta imposes a mandatory 12-month residency requirement before citizenship can be granted (reduced from 36 months for higher investment tiers)
  • The due diligence process is among the most exhaustive in the world, involving Europol-level checks and multi-jurisdictional financial screening
  • The Maltese government intentionally limits the number of approvals per year to maintain program integrity

Realistic expectation in 2026: 14–18 months minimum. Some complex applications can take up to 24 months.

Best for: Ultra-high-net-worth individuals who want the most powerful CBI passport available and are willing to invest the time and capital to obtain it. This is the program for those playing the long game.

🇵🇹 Portugal Golden Visa: The Long Road to EU Citizenship

Portugal's Golden Visa isn't a CBI program in the traditional sense — it's a residency-by-investment program with a pathway to citizenship after five years. But it remains one of the most popular routes to European status for investors globally.

What drives the timeline:

  • The initial residency card has been subject to significant processing delays, particularly since the 2023 program restructuring that eliminated real estate as a qualifying investment
  • Fund-based investments (the primary remaining route) require additional layers of regulatory approval
  • The path to citizenship requires maintaining residency status for five years, passing a basic Portuguese language test, and demonstrating ties to the country

Realistic expectation in 2026:

  • Residency card: 18–24 months from application (some reports suggest longer)
  • Citizenship eligibility: 5 years after residency is granted
  • Total timeline to passport: Approximately 7+ years from initial application

Best for: Investors who are thinking generationally — those who want EU residency rights now and are willing to commit to a multi-year pathway to one of the world's strongest passports.

🇹🇷 Turkey: The Bridge Between East and West

Turkey's CBI program offers a unique geographic and strategic position, plus access to a large and growing economy. The $400,000 real estate investment route remains the most popular.

What drives the timeline:

  • Property acquisition and title transfer processes can be time-consuming depending on the specific transaction
  • Government processing has improved but remains subject to bureaucratic variability
  • Applications involving complex family structures or multiple dependents tend to take longer

Best for: Investors who want a strong passport with good Middle Eastern and Asian travel access, plus the opportunity to hold appreciating real estate in a major emerging market.

What Actually Slows Down Your Application

Across every program we work with at Meridian Advisory, the same factors cause the majority of delays. Most of them are entirely preventable:

1. Incomplete Documentation

This is the number one cause of delays, bar none. A missing bank statement, an improperly notarized document, or an expired police clearance certificate can add weeks or even months to your timeline. Every program has specific documentary requirements, and they are unforgiving about compliance.

2. Complex Source-of-Funds Narratives

Due diligence teams need to trace your investment capital to legitimate sources. If your wealth comes from a straightforward salary or business sale, this is simple. If it comes from a combination of crypto gains, multiple business interests across different jurisdictions, and inherited assets — it takes longer. Significantly longer.

3. Dual or Multiple Nationalities

Applicants holding passports from certain countries face enhanced due diligence. This isn't discrimination — it's a reflection of international compliance requirements. Being prepared for this and providing comprehensive documentation upfront can prevent delays.

4. Family Application Complexity

Adding a spouse, children, parents, or siblings (where permitted) multiplies the documentation requirements and due diligence checks. Each dependent is essentially a separate mini-application.

5. Choosing the Wrong Authorized Agent

CBI applications must be submitted through government-authorized agents. The quality of your agent directly impacts your processing speed. An experienced agent knows exactly what the CIU is looking for, formats everything correctly, and has established relationships that help move things along.

So Which Program Is Right for You?

The answer depends on a matrix of factors that are unique to your situation:

  • How quickly do you need it? → St. Kitts (AAP) or Vanuatu
  • Do you need U.S. market access? → Grenada (E-2 visa eligibility)
  • Is EU citizenship the goal? → Malta (direct) or Portugal (long-term)
  • Are you optimizing for value? → Dominica
  • Do you want real estate as part of the investment? → Turkey or St. Kitts (real estate option)
  • Is this about legacy and generational planning? → Portugal or Malta

There is no single "best" program. There is only the best program for you.
