CBI Processing Times Compared: How Long Does It Really Take to Get a Second Passport in 2026?

June 22, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

By Meridian Advisory | Updated June 2026

One of the first questions every prospective applicant asks is deceptively simple: "How long will this take?"

It's also one of the most important. Whether you're structuring an exit from a volatile market, planning international school enrollment for your children, or simply want a Plan B before the next global disruption, timing matters. A second citizenship is only useful if it arrives when you need it.

The problem? Processing times vary dramatically — not just between programs, but between applicants. Government backlogs, due diligence complexity, the investment route you choose, and even your nationality of origin can shift a timeline by months.

We've compiled the most current, real-world data from our client pipeline and official government disclosures as of mid-2026. No theoretical ranges. No outdated estimates. Here's what you can actually expect.

Tier 1: The Speed Leaders (Under 90 Days)

🇻🇺 Vanuatu — The Fastest Passport on Earth

Typical timeline: 30–60 days

Vanuatu's Development Support Program (DSP) remains the undisputed speed champion in 2026. We've seen approvals come through in as few as 30 days from submission to passport in hand.

Why it's fast:

  • Streamlined government committee (single approval body)
  • Smaller application volume means less backlog
  • No interview or physical visit required

The trade-off: Vanuatu's passport provides visa-free access to roughly 100 destinations, including the UK and Schengen zone. However, it doesn't carry the same diplomatic weight as a Caribbean or European passport. It also doesn't include US E-2 treaty investor visa eligibility — a dealbreaker for some.

Best for: Applicants who need a second passport immediately — whether for travel flexibility, personal security, or as a bridge while a longer-term program processes.

🇰🇳 St. Kitts & Nevis — The Gold Standard, Now Faster Than Ever

Typical timeline: 60–90 days

St. Kitts operates the world's oldest CBI program (established 1984), and in 2026, it remains one of the most efficient. The standard track averages 60–90 days, while the Accelerated Application Process (AAP) can compress that to approximately 45 days.

Why it's fast:

  • Decades of institutional experience processing applications
  • Well-resourced Citizenship by Investment Unit (CIU)
  • Clear, predictable due diligence protocols
  • The AAP track was specifically designed for time-sensitive applicants

Key detail: The Accelerated track carries an additional government fee (currently $25,000 for a single applicant). For most of our clients, the premium is easily justified by the time saved.

Best for: Entrepreneurs and investors who want the strongest Caribbean passport with the fastest turnaround. The St. Kitts passport offers visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 155+ destinations, including the UK, EU Schengen area, and Singapore.

Tier 2: The 90–120 Day Window

🇬🇩 Grenada — Speed Plus US Access

Typical timeline: 90–120 days

Grenada consistently processes applications within three to four months. What makes it exceptional isn't raw speed — it's what comes with that passport.

Grenada is the only Caribbean CBI country with access to the US E-2 Treaty Investor Visa. This means a Grenadian passport holder can apply to live and work in the United States by making a qualifying business investment — a pathway that doesn't exist for citizens of most other nations.

Why this timeline holds steady:

  • The Grenada CBI Committee meets regularly and has a well-defined review process
  • Application volumes are high but manageable
  • Due diligence is thorough but efficient

Best for: Anyone who wants Caribbean citizenship and a viable pathway to the US. We consistently recommend Grenada to tech founders, e-commerce operators, and investors who want optionality in North America.

🇩🇲 Dominica — The Most Affordable, Reliably Processed

Typical timeline: 90–120 days

Dominica offers the lowest entry point of any established CBI program at $200,000 (Sustainable Growth Fund contribution for a single applicant). Processing is reliable and predictable, generally falling within the 90–120 day window.

Worth noting: Dominica has invested heavily in its due diligence infrastructure in recent years, working with international vetting agencies. This has kept the program's reputation strong without meaningfully slowing down approvals.

Best for: Cost-conscious applicants who want a well-regarded Caribbean passport without overpaying. Dominica's passport provides visa-free access to 140+ destinations, including the UK and Schengen zone.

🇦🇬 Antigua & Barbuda — Family-Friendly Processing

Typical timeline: 90–120 days

Antigua's processing speed is comparable to Dominica and Grenada. Where it differentiates is its family-friendly structure — the program allows inclusion of dependent children up to age 30 and dependent parents over 55, making it one of the most generous for multi-generational applications.

A practical note on timelines: Family applications with four or more dependents can push toward the longer end of the range (closer to 120 days) simply due to the additional due diligence checks required for each individual.

Best for: Families. If you're applying with a spouse, children, and parents, Antigua's cost structure and inclusive policies often make it the most economical choice per person.

Tier 3: The 3–6 Month Programs

🇹🇷 Turkey — Underrated and Accelerating

Typical timeline: 3–6 months

Turkey's CBI program requires a $400,000 real estate investment (held for three years) and has matured considerably. Processing has become more predictable in 2026, with most applications completing in three to six months.

Why it takes longer than Caribbean programs:

  • Real estate transactions involve title deed transfers, valuations, and notarization
  • The Turkish General Directorate of Population and Citizenship Affairs handles approvals centrally
  • Additional security screening applies to certain nationalities

The upside: A Turkish passport offers visa-free access to 110+ countries and, critically, E-2 treaty eligibility with the United States — the same advantage as Grenada, but through a real estate pathway rather than a donation.

Best for: Investors who want tangible real estate assets alongside citizenship. Istanbul's property market continues to attract serious capital, making this a dual-purpose play.

Tier 4: The Long Game (12+ Months)

🇲🇹 Malta — The European Gold Standard

Typical timeline: 12–14 months (minimum 12 months by law)

Malta's Exceptional Investor Naturalisation policy is the only pathway to direct EU citizenship through investment remaining in 2026. And the Maltese government takes its time deliberately.

A 12-month minimum residency requirement is built into the statute. No exceptions. No accelerated track. This is by design — Malta wants to demonstrate to the European Commission that its program is rigorous, selective, and not a rubber stamp.

What happens during those 12+ months:

  • Months 1–2: Application submission, government acknowledgment
  • Months 3–6: Extensive four-tier due diligence (including field investigations)
  • Months 6–12: Residency requirement fulfilled, final review
  • Months 12–14: Oath of allegiance, passport issuance

The investment: A minimum contribution of €690,000 to the national development fund (reduced to €600,000 after 36 months of residency), plus a €700,000+ property purchase or €16,000/year rental, plus a €10,000 philanthropic donation.

Total outlay: typically €1.1M–€1.5M+ for a family.

Best for: Ultra-high-net-worth individuals who want full EU citizenship — not just residency. A Maltese passport grants the right to live, work, and do business anywhere in the European Union. Visa-free access to 190+ destinations, including the US.

🇵🇹 Portugal Golden Visa — Residency First, Citizenship Later

Typical timeline: 12–18 months for residency permit; 5 years to citizenship eligibility

Portugal's Golden Visa is not technically a CBI program — it's a residency-by-investment pathway that leads to citizenship after five years. But it remains one of the most popular programs among our clients, and for good reason.

The 2026 landscape: Following the 2023 reforms that eliminated real estate as a qualifying investment route, the program now focuses on fund subscriptions (€500,000 minimum), company creation (10+ jobs), and cultural/research contributions. Processing for the initial residency card currently runs 12–18 months, reflecting ongoing demand and administrative adjustments.

The full timeline to Portuguese (EU) citizenship:

1. Months 0–18: Golden Visa residency approved

2. Years 1–5: Maintain residency status (minimum 7 days/year in Portugal)

3. Year 5: Apply for permanent residency or citizenship

4. Year 5–6: Citizenship processing and approval

Total time to passport: approximately 6–7 years.

Best for: Families who want EU residency now and are willing to play the long game for full citizenship. Portugal's quality of life, NHR (now IFICI) tax regime, and Golden Visa flexibility make it an unmatched lifestyle-plus-investment combination.

What Actually Causes Delays?

In our experience managing hundreds of applications, these are the most common bottlenecks — and they're almost all avoidable:

1. Incomplete Documentation

Impact: +30–60 days

Missing bank statements, improperly notarized documents, or expired police clearance certificates are the #1 cause of preventable delays. Every resubmission resets a portion of the review clock.

2. Complex Source-of-Funds Narratives

Impact: +30–90 days

If your wealth comes from multiple streams — equity exits, crypto gains, real estate portfolios, inheritance — expect the due diligence team to ask follow-up questions. This isn't a problem; it just requires preparation. We work with clients to build an airtight source-of-funds narrative before submission.

3. High-Risk Nationality Flags

Impact: +30–60 days

Applicants from certain jurisdictions face enhanced due diligence by default. This is a reality of the global compliance landscape. It doesn't disqualify you — but it does mean your application will receive additional scrutiny from third-party vetting agencies.

4. Real Estate Transaction Complications

Impact: +30–90 days (Turkey, Portugal, Malta)

Programs that require property investment introduce a second variable: the real estate transaction itself. Title issues, valuation disputes, and escrow delays can all extend your overall timeline independently of the government's processing speed.

5. Government Backlogs and Policy Shifts

Impact: Unpredictable

Governments adjust. Programs get restructured. Quotas get introduced. In 2026, we're watching several programs closely for potential regulatory changes that could affect processing capacity. Starting sooner rather than later is almost always the right move.

Which Program Fits Your Timeline?

Here's a simplified decision framework:

"I need a passport in 30–60 days."

→ Vanuatu, St. Kitts (Accelerated)

"I can wait 3–4 months for the right passport."

→ Grenada (especially if US access matters), Dominica (if budget matters), St. Kitts (standard track)

"I want EU citizenship and I'm willing to invest significantly."

→ Malta (12–14 months to full EU citizenship)

→ Portugal Golden Visa (residency now, citizenship in 5–6 years)

"I want real estate and citizenship together."

→ Turkey (3–6 months), St. Kitts or Grenada (real estate option, 60–120 days)
