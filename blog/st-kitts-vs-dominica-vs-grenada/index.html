St. Kitts vs. Dominica vs. Grenada: Which Caribbean Passport Should You Choose?

March 2026 · 10 min read

The three most popular Caribbean CBI programs — St. Kitts and Nevis, Dominica, and Grenada — each offer lifetime citizenship with no residency requirement. At first glance, they look similar. But the differences matter, and the right choice depends entirely on what you're trying to accomplish.

Here's an honest, side-by-side breakdown.

Investment Requirements

St. Kitts and Nevis: $250,000 minimum contribution to the Sustainable Island State Contribution (SISC) fund. Real estate option starts at $325,000+.

Dominica: $200,000 minimum contribution to the Economic Diversification Fund (EDF). Real estate option starts at $200,000.

Grenada: $235,000 minimum contribution to the National Transformation Fund (NTF). Real estate option starts at $270,000.

Bottom line: Dominica is the most affordable. St. Kitts is the most expensive. Grenada sits in the middle but offers something the others can't — which we'll get to.

Visa-Free Travel Access

St. Kitts and Nevis: 156 countries — the strongest Caribbean passport for travel.

Grenada: 148 countries, plus E-2 treaty access to the United States.

Dominica: 140+ countries.

All three provide visa-free access to the UK, EU Schengen zone, Singapore, Hong Kong, and most of Latin America and the Caribbean.

Bottom line: If raw travel numbers matter most, St. Kitts wins. But Grenada's E-2 treaty adds a dimension that passport rankings don't capture.

The Grenada E-2 Factor

This is Grenada's defining advantage. As the only Caribbean CBI nation with a US E-2 Investor Visa Treaty, Grenada citizens can apply to live and work in the United States by investing in a US-based business. Neither St. Kitts nor Dominica offers anything comparable.

If US access is any part of your planning — whether for business, education, or quality of life — Grenada is the clear choice among these three.

Processing Times

All three programs currently process applications in approximately three to six months. Historically, St. Kitts offered an accelerated processing option for an additional fee, but standard timelines across all programs have converged.

Bottom line: No meaningful difference here in 2026.

Family Inclusion

St. Kitts: Spouse, children under 25, parents over 65.

Dominica: Spouse, children under 30, parents and grandparents over 65.

Grenada: Spouse, children under 30, parents over 55, and siblings under 18.

Bottom line: Dominica and Grenada are more generous with dependent age limits. If you have adult children in their late 20s or parents between 55-65, check the specific eligibility carefully.

Due Diligence and Reputation

All three programs conduct background checks on applicants, but the rigor and international perception vary.

St. Kitts, as the oldest program, has invested heavily in its reputation and compliance standards. Dominica is widely regarded as having thorough, consistent due diligence. Grenada's program benefits from its E-2 treaty relationship with the US, which inherently requires a higher standard of vetting.

With ECCIRA's regional harmonization efforts in 2026, all Caribbean programs are moving toward standardized compliance. This is good news for investors across the board.

Real Estate Options

If you're interested in the real estate route rather than a donation, the experience differs:

St. Kitts: The most mature real estate market among the three, with a range of approved hotel and resort developments. Properties can be resold after seven years to a future CBI applicant.

Dominica: A growing but smaller selection of approved projects. Property can be resold after three years.

Grenada: Approved real estate options with a five-year hold period. The real estate route is slightly more expensive but comes with the E-2 treaty benefit built in.

Our Recommendation Framework

Choose St. Kitts if

  • Maximum visa-free travel is your top priority
  • You value the strongest brand recognition and longest track record
  • You prefer the most established real estate options
  • Budget is less of a concern

Choose Dominica if

  • You want the most cost-effective Caribbean citizenship
  • You need a straightforward, well-run process
  • US access is not a priority
  • You want generous dependent age limits

Choose Grenada if

  • You want or may want US access through the E-2 visa
  • You're an entrepreneur considering US business expansion
  • Your family includes parents between 55-65 or siblings under 18
  • You want a balance of travel access and strategic positioning

The Honest Truth

There's no universally "best" Caribbean passport. There's only the best passport for your situation. We've seen clients who were initially set on one program switch to another after a thorough assessment of their goals. The investment difference between these programs is $15,000-$50,000 — meaningful, but not the deciding factor when you're making a lifetime decision.

What matters most is matching the right program to your goals, family situation, and long-term plans. That's what we help with.
