Türkiye Citizenship by Investment Through Real Estate: Your Complete 2026 Guide

May 04, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

How to Obtain a Turkish Passport Through Property Investment — Updated for 2026

Why Türkiye Is One of the Most Compelling CBI Options in 2026

Türkiye has quietly become one of the most strategically valuable citizenship-by-investment destinations in the world. Sitting at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, offering visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to over 110 countries, and boasting one of the fastest-growing real estate markets among G20 nations — Türkiye's citizenship program delivers an exceptional combination of affordability, speed, and global mobility.

For entrepreneurs, investors, and globally mobile professionals looking to diversify their citizenship portfolio in 2026, Türkiye deserves serious consideration. Here's everything you need to know.

The Basics: How Türkiye's Real Estate CBI Program Works

Türkiye's citizenship-by-investment program through real estate is governed by Turkish Citizenship Law No. 5901 and was restructured in 2022 to set the current investment thresholds. Here are the key parameters as of 2026:

| Requirement | Details |

|---|---|

| Minimum Investment | $400,000 USD in real estate |

| Holding Period | 3 years (property cannot be sold) |

| Eligible Property Types | Residential, commercial, land, or mixed-use |

| Number of Properties | One or multiple (must total $400,000+) |

| Family Inclusion | Spouse and children under 18 |

| Processing Time | Approximately 3–6 months |

| Dual Citizenship | Fully permitted — no need to renounce existing citizenship |

Key Points to Note:

  • The property must be purchased from a Turkish citizen or a Turkish company. Purchases between foreign nationals do not qualify.
  • The property valuation is conducted by a government-licensed appraiser — the assessed value, not just the purchase price, must meet the $400,000 threshold.
  • A deed restriction (annotation) is placed on the title deed confirming the 3-year holding commitment.

Why HNW Individuals Are Choosing Türkiye in 2026

1. Powerful Passport, Growing Global Reach

The Turkish passport currently provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 110+ destinations, including Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Thailand, most of South America, and the entirety of Central Asia. Türkiye's ongoing diplomatic expansion means this number has been steadily climbing — and with EU accession discussions periodically resurfacing, the long-term trajectory is promising.

2. Genuine Real Estate Upside

Unlike some CBI programs where the "investment" is essentially a sunk cost or donation, Turkish real estate offers real asset appreciation potential. Istanbul, in particular, has seen property values increase significantly over the past several years, driven by:

  • Massive infrastructure projects (Istanbul Canal, new metro lines, urban regeneration zones)
  • A booming tourism sector (Türkiye welcomed over 60 million tourists in recent years)
  • Favorable exchange rate dynamics for foreign buyers
  • Growing demand from international remote workers and digital nomads

Cities like Istanbul, Antalya, Bodrum, and Ankara each offer distinct investment profiles, from high-yield rental properties to luxury coastal developments.

3. Speed of Processing

Compared to European programs that can take 12–24+ months, Türkiye's program is remarkably fast. Many applicants receive their Turkish ID and passport within 3 to 6 months of filing — one of the quickest turnarounds in the global CBI landscape.

4. No Residency Requirement

This is a critical differentiator. Türkiye does not require you to live in the country to obtain or maintain citizenship. There's no minimum stay requirement — you invest, you receive citizenship, and you're free to live anywhere in the world.

5. Strategic Tax Planning Opportunities

Türkiye operates on a territorial-leaning tax system for non-residents. If you become a Turkish citizen but do not establish tax residency in Türkiye (i.e., you don't spend 183+ days per year there), you are generally not taxed on your worldwide income by the Turkish government. This makes it an attractive option for investors seeking to optimize their global tax position — particularly those in crypto, e-commerce, or location-independent businesses.

6. Gateway to Business in Emerging Markets

A Turkish passport opens doors not just for travel, but for business access across Central Asia, the Middle East, and Africa — regions where Türkiye has deep economic ties and where Turkish business credentials carry significant weight. For entrepreneurs looking to expand into these markets, Turkish citizenship is a strategic asset, not just a travel document.

The Step-by-Step Process

Here's what the journey looks like from initial decision to passport in hand:

Step 1: Initial Consultation & Strategy

Work with an experienced advisory firm (like Meridian Advisory) to determine whether Türkiye aligns with your mobility goals, tax strategy, and investment preferences. We help you understand how Turkish citizenship fits within a broader second-citizenship portfolio.

Step 2: Property Selection

Identify qualifying properties. This can be done remotely or during an in-country visit. We recommend working with vetted real estate partners who specialize in CBI-qualifying properties to ensure:

  • The property meets the $400,000 valuation threshold
  • The seller is a Turkish citizen or Turkish-registered company
  • The property is free of encumbrances or legal issues

Step 3: Government Appraisal

A licensed appraiser assesses the property's value. This is a mandatory step — the government's valuation must confirm the minimum investment threshold is met.

Step 4: Purchase & Title Deed Transfer

The property purchase is finalized at the Land Registry Office (Tapu). A deed restriction is annotated, confirming the 3-year holding commitment.

Step 5: Residency Permit Application

A short-term residency permit is obtained as a procedural step. This does not require you to live in Türkiye — it's simply part of the citizenship application pathway.

Step 6: Citizenship Application

The full citizenship application is submitted to the General Directorate of Civil Registration and Nationality. Required documents typically include:

  • Valid passport and copies
  • Birth certificate (apostilled)
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable, apostilled)
  • Passport photos
  • Title deed with annotation
  • Property appraisal report
  • Proof of payment / bank transfer records

Step 7: Approval & Passport Issuance

Upon approval, you and your eligible family members receive Turkish citizenship. You can then apply for your Turkish passport and national ID card.

What Can Go Wrong? Common Pitfalls to Avoid

The program is straightforward, but there are traps that catch unprepared investors:

  • Overpaying for undervalued property. Some developers inflate prices for foreign CBI buyers. Always insist on an independent appraisal and market comparison.
  • Buying from another foreigner. This disqualifies the investment entirely. Verify seller nationality before proceeding.
  • Currency fluctuation miscalculation. The $400,000 threshold is in USD. With the Turkish lira's volatility, ensure the USD equivalent is locked in at the time of transfer.
  • Incomplete document apostilles. Missing or improperly apostilled documents are the #1 cause of processing delays.
  • Neglecting due diligence on the property. Ensure there are no existing liens, debts, or zoning violations tied to the property.

Working with a knowledgeable CBI advisory firm eliminates virtually all of these risks.

The E-2 Visa Advantage

One of Türkiye's most underappreciated benefits: it is an E-2 Treaty country with the United States. This means Turkish citizens can apply for a US E-2 Investor Visa, which allows them to live and work in the United States by investing in a US-based business. For investors who want US access without the lengthy and uncertain EB-5 process, the Türkiye → E-2 pathway is one of the most efficient routes available in 2026.

Grenada is the only Caribbean CBI program that also offers E-2 access, making Türkiye and Grenada the two leading options for investors with US ambitions.
