Vanuatu Citizenship by Investment: The Fastest Passport in the World (2026 Guide)

April 20, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Processing time: as little as 30–60 days. No interview. No residency requirement. No visit required.

If speed is your priority, no citizenship by investment program on Earth moves faster than Vanuatu.

While most CBI programs take anywhere from 3 to 12 months — and some European pathways stretch into years — the Republic of Vanuatu has built its Development Support Program (DSP) around one principle: efficiency without compromise.

For high-net-worth individuals navigating an increasingly complex global landscape, Vanuatu has quietly become one of the most strategic second citizenship options available in 2026.

Let's break down everything you need to know.

What Is the Vanuatu DSP?

The Development Support Program (DSP) is Vanuatu's official citizenship by investment pathway, established under the Citizenship Act and regulated by the Vanuatu Financial Services Commission (VFSC) and the Citizenship Commission.

Unlike residency-by-investment schemes that require years of physical presence before naturalization, the DSP grants full, irrevocable citizenship — including a passport — through a direct economic contribution to the country's development fund.

No residency. No language test. No interview. No renunciation of your existing citizenship.

Why Vanuatu? The Strategic Case

⚡ Speed That's Unmatched

The headline stat is real. Most applicants receive approval within 30 days and hold their passport within 45–60 days from submission. In a world where Malta takes 12–14 months and Portugal can stretch to 2+ years for full citizenship, Vanuatu operates in a different category entirely.

🌍 Visa-Free Travel to 90+ Countries

A Vanuatu passport provides visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to approximately 90+ destinations, including:

  • United Kingdom (6-month visa-free access)
  • Schengen Area (90 days within 180-day periods) — pending ETIAS implementation
  • Hong Kong
  • Singapore
  • Russia
  • Most of the Caribbean, Africa, and the Pacific Islands

For entrepreneurs and investors who need to move quickly across borders — particularly into the UK and Europe — this is a powerful tool.

🏦 Zero Personal Income Tax

Vanuatu levies no personal income tax, no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, and no withholding tax. It is one of the few genuinely zero-tax jurisdictions in the world.

For crypto investors, digital entrepreneurs, and those restructuring their global tax footprint, Vanuatu citizenship can be a cornerstone of a broader wealth-planning strategy.

> Important note: Tax obligations depend on your country of residence and overall structure. Citizenship alone doesn't eliminate tax liability in your home country. Always consult a qualified tax advisor.

🤝 Dual Citizenship Allowed

Vanuatu fully recognizes dual (and multiple) citizenship. You are not required to renounce your existing nationality. Your current passport, rights, and residency status remain untouched.

Investment Requirements (2026)

The Vanuatu DSP requires a non-refundable contribution to the national development fund. Here's the current fee structure:

| Applicant Type | Contribution Amount (USD) |

|---|---|

| Single Applicant | ~$130,000 |

| Couple | ~$150,000 |

| Family of 4 (spouse + 2 dependents) | ~$180,000 |

| Each Additional Dependent | ~$15,000–$25,000 |

Fees are approximate and subject to change. Government processing fees, due diligence fees, and agent fees apply in addition to the contribution. We provide exact, transparent cost breakdowns during consultation.

Eligible Dependents

  • Spouse
  • Children under 25 (in some cases, up to 30 if financially dependent)
  • Parents over 50 (financially dependent)

The Application Process: Step by Step

Total timeline: approximately 30–60 days

Step 1: Initial Consultation & Eligibility Assessment

We review your background, goals, and family situation to confirm eligibility and map out the optimal path.

Step 2: Document Preparation

Gather required documentation — passport copies, police clearance certificates, proof of funds, bank reference letters, medical certificates, and personal declarations.

Step 3: Submission & Due Diligence

Your application is submitted to the Vanuatu Citizenship Commission. A thorough due diligence process is conducted, including international background checks.

Step 4: Approval

Most approvals are granted within 30 days of submission. You'll receive an approval-in-principle letter.

Step 5: Contribution Payment

Upon approval, the contribution to the development fund is made.

Step 6: Oath of Allegiance & Passport Issuance

You take an oath of allegiance (this can often be done at a Vanuatu consulate or authorized location — no travel to Vanuatu required). Your passport and citizenship certificate are then issued and delivered.

That's it. From first consultation to passport in hand, the entire process can take as little as 5–8 weeks.

Common Questions

Is Vanuatu citizenship internationally recognized?

Yes. Vanuatu is a sovereign nation, a member of the United Nations, the Commonwealth of Nations, and numerous international bodies. Its passport is a fully legitimate travel document.

Will I need to live in Vanuatu?

No. There is zero residency requirement — not before, during, or after obtaining citizenship.

Can I open international bank accounts with a Vanuatu passport?

Yes, though banking due diligence standards vary by institution and jurisdiction. We advise clients on banking strategies as part of our consultation.

Is the due diligence process rigorous?

Yes, and it should be. Vanuatu has strengthened its due diligence procedures significantly in recent years. This is a feature, not a bug — it protects the integrity of the program and the value of your new citizenship.

Is Vanuatu likely to close or change the program?

CBI programs evolve. We always recommend acting on current terms rather than speculating. The DSP has been operational for several years and remains a key revenue stream for the Vanuatu government, but no program is guaranteed to stay open indefinitely.
