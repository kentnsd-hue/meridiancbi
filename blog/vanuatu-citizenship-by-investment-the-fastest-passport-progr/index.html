Vanuatu Citizenship by Investment: The Fastest Passport Program in the World

May 31, 2026 | Meridian Advisory

Published by Meridian Advisory | 2026

30 Days to a Second Passport. No, That's Not a Typo.

In a world where most citizenship by investment programs take anywhere from three to twelve months to process, Vanuatu stands in a league of its own. The Vanuatu Development Support Program (DSP) can deliver a fully legal second citizenship — passport in hand — in as little as 30 to 60 days.

For entrepreneurs, investors, and globally mobile professionals operating in an increasingly uncertain geopolitical landscape, that speed isn't just convenient. It's a strategic advantage.

Let's break down everything you need to know about the Vanuatu CBI program in 2026 — the costs, the benefits, the limitations, and who it's really for.

What Is the Vanuatu DSP?

The Development Support Program (DSP) is Vanuatu's official citizenship by investment pathway, established under the Citizenship Act [CAP 112]. It allows foreign nationals to obtain irrevocable citizenship in the Republic of Vanuatu through a one-time financial contribution to the country's development fund.

Vanuatu is a South Pacific island nation made up of roughly 80 islands, located east of Australia. It's a parliamentary republic, a member of the Commonwealth of Nations, and — critically for CBI applicants — a country with zero personal income tax, zero capital gains tax, zero inheritance tax, and zero wealth tax.

Yes, you read that correctly. Zero across the board.

Why Speed Matters in 2026

The global mobility landscape has shifted dramatically. Tax policy changes across the EU, increasing CRS reporting requirements, rising geopolitical tensions, and a growing trend toward capital controls have pushed high-net-worth individuals to prioritize optionality.

A second passport is no longer a luxury. It's a hedge.

And when the window of opportunity is narrow — whether you're navigating a business exit, restructuring international holdings, or simply want a Plan B secured before regulatory environments shift further — processing time becomes a critical variable.

Investment Requirements

The Vanuatu DSP is structured as a non-refundable government contribution — not a real estate purchase or business investment. This makes it one of the most straightforward programs from a structural standpoint.

Contribution Thresholds (2026):

  • Single applicant: Starting from approximately USD $130,000
  • Married couple: Starting from approximately USD $150,000
  • Family of four: Starting from approximately USD $180,000

Note: Fees include government contribution, due diligence fees, and processing costs. Exact figures may vary based on family composition and application specifics. We always recommend verifying the latest fee schedule during your consultation.

Additional costs to factor in:

  • Legal and advisory fees
  • Document authentication and apostille costs
  • Passport issuance fees
  • Optional travel to Vanuatu (not required for the application)

No physical residency is required — not before, during, or after the application. There is no interview. There is no language requirement. There is no minimum stay obligation.

The Vanuatu Passport: What Does It Actually Get You?

This is where applicants need to think critically. The Vanuatu passport is powerful in certain contexts and limited in others. Let's be transparent about both.

✅ Strengths

Visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 90+ destinations, including:

  • United Kingdom
  • Russia
  • Hong Kong
  • Singapore (30-day visa-free)
  • Most of the EU Schengen Area (up to 90 days within a 180-day period)
  • Most of the Caribbean, Africa, and the Pacific Islands

Tax residence advantages:

  • No personal income tax
  • No corporate tax on foreign-sourced income
  • No capital gains, inheritance, or wealth tax
  • Not part of most automatic tax information exchange networks (though this is evolving)

Commonwealth membership provides certain travel and diplomatic courtesies.

Dual citizenship is fully permitted — Vanuatu does not require you to renounce your existing nationality.

⚠️ Limitations

  • No visa-free access to the United States, Canada, or Australia. If these are your primary travel destinations, Vanuatu alone won't solve that. However, it can be an excellent component of a broader multi-passport strategy.
  • No direct E-2 treaty with the United States. Unlike Grenada, Vanuatu does not offer a pathway to a U.S. investor visa.
  • Perception considerations. Some banking institutions may require additional documentation from Vanuatu passport holders. Working with the right advisory team mitigates this significantly.
  • Evolving EU relationship. Vanuatu's Schengen visa-free access has faced scrutiny in recent years. While access has been maintained, applicants should stay informed about any policy shifts — something we actively monitor for all our clients.

You already hold one or two passports but want to add a third from a completely different geopolitical sphere. Vanuatu, as a Pacific Island Commonwealth nation, offers genuine geopolitical diversification.

5. The Privacy-Minded Individual

While no CBI program offers true anonymity — nor should it — Vanuatu's streamlined process and geographic separation from major economic blocs appeal to those who value discretion in their personal and financial affairs.

The Application Process: Step by Step

Here's what a typical Vanuatu DSP timeline looks like when you work with Meridian Advisory:

Week 1–2: Initial Consultation & Document Preparation

We assess your eligibility, discuss your goals, and begin compiling the required documentation — passport copies, police clearances, financial references, and medical certificates.

Week 2–3: Application Submission

Your completed application is submitted to the Vanuatu Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) and the Citizenship Commission for due diligence review.

Week 3–5: Due Diligence & Background Checks

The government conducts its review. Vanuatu has significantly strengthened its due diligence processes in recent years to maintain program integrity and international credibility.

Week 5–7: Approval & Oath of Allegiance

Upon approval, you take the Oath of Allegiance. This can typically be done at a Vanuatu diplomatic mission or authorized location — travel to Vanuatu itself is not required.

Week 6–8: Passport Issuance

Your Vanuatu passport is issued and delivered. You are now a citizen of the Republic of Vanuatu.

Total elapsed time: 30 to 60 days in most cases.

Common Concerns (Addressed Honestly)

"Is Vanuatu citizenship legitimate?"

Yes. The DSP is a fully codified government program under Vanuatu's Citizenship Act. It is not a residency-by-investment scheme or a gray-market arrangement. You receive full, irrevocable citizenship and a biometric passport.

"Will banks accept a Vanuatu passport?"

Tier-1 international banks may require additional documentation, but with proper structuring and advisory support, banking access is achievable. This is one of the areas where working with an experienced firm like Meridian Advisory makes a material difference.

"Can the citizenship be revoked?"

Citizenship granted under the DSP is irrevocable under Vanuatu law, provided the application was made truthfully and in good faith. Misrepresentation during the application process is the only basis for revocation.

"Do I have to live in Vanuatu?"

No. There is no residency requirement whatsoever. Most DSP citizens never set foot on the islands — though if you ever visit, the diving is world-class.

The Bottom Line

Vanuatu's DSP isn't the most powerful passport in the world measured purely by visa-free destinations. It doesn't unlock U.S. residency. It won't replace a Maltese or Portuguese passport for someone who needs deep EU integration.

But that's not what it's designed to do.

What Vanuatu offers is unmatched speed, simplicity, cost-efficiency, and tax neutrality — wrapped in a legitimate, Commonwealth-backed citizenship. For the right individual, at the right moment, those four factors matter more than anything else on the comparison chart.

In a world that's moving faster and becoming less predictable, the ability to secure a second citizenship in 30 days isn't just impressive. It's insurance.
